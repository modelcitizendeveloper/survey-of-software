## Executive Summary

Commercial kitchen production scheduling sits at the intersection of **manufacturing MRP** (Katana, MRPeasy, BatchMaster) and **food-specific software** (FlexiBake, BakeSmart, Craftybase). The market shows clear segmentation:

- **Micro operations** (1-3 people): Spreadsheets or very simple tools ($0-50/mo)
- **Small bakeries/catering** (5-20 people): Food-specific platforms ($95-300/mo)
- **Medium operations** (20-100 people): General MRP with food features ($300-800/mo)
- **Enterprise** (100+ people): Full ERP systems (custom pricing)

**Key Finding**: Most commercial kitchens **don't buy production scheduling software** - they use spreadsheets, POS order management, or general project management tools. Dedicated production scheduling adoption is low outside wholesale bakeries and CPG manufacturers.

### Category 4: Non-Specialist Alternatives

Many commercial kitchens **don't buy dedicated production scheduling software**. Instead:

#### Spreadsheets (Excel/Google Sheets)
- **Pricing**: Free (Excel with Microsoft 365) or free (Google Sheets)
- **Approach**: Custom templates for production planning
- **Pros**: No learning curve, infinite customization, no monthly fees
- **Cons**: Manual updates, no automation, error-prone, doesn't scale
- **Best For**: Micro operations (1-5 employees), temporary use while evaluating platforms

---

#### Project Management Tools (ClickUp, Monday.com, Asana)
- **Pricing**: $0-25/user/month
- **Approach**: Repurpose task/project management for production workflows
- **Pros**: Visual boards, team collaboration, mobile apps, affordable
- **Cons**: Not designed for manufacturing, no recipe integration, no costing
- **Best For**: Small catering companies, meal prep services needing workflow visibility
- **Examples**:
  - ClickUp with manufacturing templates
  - Monday.com with custom production boards
  - Notion with custom databases

---

#### POS Systems with Production Features
- **Pricing**: Included with POS ($50-200/mo)
- **Platforms**: Toast, Square, Lightspeed
- **Approach**: Use order forecasting and basic production planning features
- **Pros**: Already paying for POS, order data feeds production planning
- **Cons**: Very basic production scheduling, no advanced features
- **Best For**: Restaurants doing prep work, bakeries with simple production

## Decision Factors

### 1. Industry Specialization
- **Bakery-only**: FlexiBake, BakeSmart, CakeBoss, Cybake
- **General food/beverage**: Katana, MRPeasy, BatchMaster, Craftybase
- **Process manufacturing**: BatchMaster (formula-based focus)

### 2. Production Model
- **Make-to-order** (catering, custom cakes): Need order-driven scheduling (FlexiBake, BakeSmart)
- **Make-to-stock** (wholesale bread, meal prep): Need inventory-driven scheduling (Katana, MRPeasy)
- **Hybrid** (retail + wholesale): Need both capabilities (FlexiBake Professional, Katana)

### 3. Complexity
- **Single product, single location**: Spreadsheets or very simple tools
- **Multiple products, single location**: BakeSmart, Craftybase, MRPeasy Starter
- **Multiple products, multiple locations**: Katana, FlexiBake, BatchMaster

### 4. Integration Needs
- **Standalone**: Can use any platform (Craftybase, CakeBoss)
- **POS integration**: FlexiBake (online ordering), BakeSmart (POS integration)
- **Accounting integration**: MRPeasy (QuickBooks), Katana (accounting systems), FlexiBake
- **Inventory integration**: All MRP platforms (Katana, MRPeasy, BatchMaster)

### 5. Budget
- **$0-50/mo**: Spreadsheets, CakeBoss ($20/year), Craftybase ($19/mo)
- **$50-300/mo**: BakeSmart ($99), MRPeasy ($49-149), Katana Starter ($179)
- **$300-800/mo**: FlexiBake ($295-375), Katana Professional ($799), Wherefour ($300-500)
- **$800+/mo or custom**: BatchMaster, Katana Pro Plus ($1799), FlexiBake Enterprise

## Key Insights

### 1. Low Adoption Outside Wholesale
Most **restaurants, small bakeries, catering companies don't buy production scheduling software**. They use:
- Spreadsheets (most common)
- POS order management
- Project management tools (ClickUp, Monday.com)
- Mental planning (very small operations)

**Why**: Production is simple enough that dedicated software ROI doesn't justify $100-800/mo cost.

---

### 2. Bakery vs General Food Manufacturing
- **Bakeries** get better ROI from bakery-specific platforms (FlexiBake, BakeSmart, Cybake)
  - Bakery workflows are standardized (mixing, proofing, baking, cooling)
  - Recipe management is critical (flour, yeast, temperature, timing)
  - Lot tracking and expiry dates are regulatory requirements

- **General food manufacturers** use general MRP (Katana, MRPeasy, BatchMaster)
  - More diverse production workflows
  - Need flexibility vs bakery-specific assumptions
  - Often need integration with contract manufacturing, co-packing

---

### 3. Price Jumps at Feature Inflection Points
- **$0-50/mo**: Very basic (spreadsheets, order tracking)
- **$50-300/mo**: Real production scheduling, but limited locations/SKUs
- **$300-800/mo**: Multi-location, unlimited SKUs, advanced features
- **$800+/mo**: Enterprise features, multiple facilities, full ERP integration

**Implication**: Most small operations jump from spreadsheets ($0) directly to mid-tier platforms ($99-300/mo), skipping the micro tools.

---

### 4. Integration is Differentiator
Platforms that integrate with:
- **Accounting** (QuickBooks, Xero): MRPeasy, Katana, FlexiBake
- **POS systems**: FlexiBake, BakeSmart
- **Inventory management**: All MRP platforms
- **eCommerce**: BakeSmart, FlexiBake

**Winner**: FlexiBake (bakery-specific) and Katana (general food) offer deepest integration ecosystems.

---

### 5. Enterprise = Custom Pricing + SAP
Enterprise food manufacturing consolidates around:
- BatchMaster (built on SAP Business One)
- Full ERP systems (SAP, Oracle, Dynamics 365 with food modules)

**Implication**: Small-medium operators avoid enterprise platforms due to cost and complexity.

---

## Gaps in Current Market

### 1. Affordable Production Scheduling for Small Catering
- **Gap**: Catering companies (5-20 employees) need multi-day production planning
- **Current Options**: Spreadsheets or repurpose project management tools
- **Missing**: $50-150/mo catering-specific production platform

### 2. Integration with Inventory Management
- **Gap**: Most platforms require buying their inventory module (expensive)
- **Current Options**: Buy full MRP/ERP or use separate systems
- **Missing**: Production scheduling that integrates with existing inventory (3.070 platforms)

### 3. Capacity Planning for Shared Equipment
- **Gap**: Commissary kitchens need equipment slot scheduling (oven #1 available 9-11am)
- **Current Options**: Google Calendar or spreadsheets
- **Missing**: Equipment capacity scheduling with production workflows

### 4. Mobile Production Tracking
- **Gap**: Kitchen staff need mobile apps to update production status
- **Current Options**: Desktop-only platforms (FlexiBake, BatchMaster) or basic mobile (Katana, MRPeasy)
- **Missing**: Mobile-first production tracking for kitchen floor

## Key Differentiators

### FlexiBake: Bakery Domain Expertise
**Strengths**:
- Best-in-class for **bakery-specific workflows** (mixing, proofing, baking cycles)
- **Nutritional analysis** and **FDA/USDA labeling compliance** built-in
- **Storage room/freezer management** (unique to bakery operations)
- **Online order portal** for wholesale customers
- **20 years** in bakery software (mature, stable)

**Weaknesses**:
- Dated user interface (feels like software from 2010s)
- Limited integration ecosystem (not many third-party connectors)
- Higher cost than general MRP ($375/mo vs $150/mo)
- Bakery-specific = not flexible for other food types

**Best For**: Wholesale bakeries, multi-location bakeries, commercial bakeries with regulatory compliance needs

---

### Katana: Modern Cloud MRP
**Strengths**:
- **Modern, intuitive interface** (visual production dashboard)
- **Unlimited users** on all plans (huge cost savings vs per-user pricing)
- **Strong integration ecosystem** (Shopify, ShipStation, accounting)
- **REST API** for custom integrations
- Good for **multi-location operations** (up to 10 locations on Professional)

**Weaknesses**:
- Not bakery-specific (no nutritional analysis, no HACCP)
- **Price increases** (2024-2025 user complaints about pricing structure changes)
- Limited equipment capacity modeling (can't model oven utilization deeply)
- Backward scheduling not as advanced as MRPeasy or BatchMaster

**Best For**: Growing food manufacturers, CPG brands scaling from 10-100 employees, multi-product food companies

---

### MRPeasy: Affordable Simplicity
**Strengths**:
- **Most affordable** real MRP ($49-149/mo vs $359-799/mo)
- **Simple, easy to implement** (2-4 weeks vs 8+ weeks)
- **QuickBooks integration** (popular among small businesses)
- **Backward scheduling with buffer days** (added Jan 2025 - ahead of Katana)
- **30 languages** (global accessibility)
- **Power BI integration** (added March 2025)

**Weaknesses**:
- Less feature-rich than competitors (no nutritional analysis, basic compliance)
- Limited customization vs enterprise platforms
- Smaller company = higher vendor risk vs established players
- Basic warehouse management (not suitable for complex multi-facility operations)

**Best For**: Small food manufacturers (10-50 employees), budget-conscious operations, simple production workflows

---

### BatchMaster: Enterprise Process Manufacturing
**Strengths**:
- **Best-in-class for process manufacturing** (formula-based production)
- **Advanced compliance** (HACCP, FDA, USDA, full traceability)
- **SAP Business One foundation** (enterprise-grade stability)
- **Side-by-side cost comparison** (compare formulations)
- **Advanced scheduling** (MPS + MRP + forecasting)
- Both **on-premises and cloud** deployment options

**Weaknesses**:
- **Expensive** (enterprise pricing, likely $2K-10K+/mo)
- **Complex implementation** (8-16 weeks minimum)
- **Steep learning curve** (requires training, dedicated staff)
- **Overkill for small operations** (`<50` employees don't need this)

**Best For**: Enterprise food/beverage manufacturers (100+ employees), complex formulations, multi-facility operations, heavy regulatory requirements

## Equipment Capacity Optimization: The $650K Question

### Wholesale Bakery Equipment Analysis
- **Current utilization**: Ovens 100% utilized 6am-2pm, 30% utilized 2pm-6pm
- **Opportunity**: Increase afternoon utilization to 60-70%
- **Additional capacity**: 30-40% more production without buying new ovens
- **Revenue opportunity**: $625K-750K/year (at current margins)

### Why Equipment Optimization Matters
- **Capital cost**: Commercial oven = $15K-50K
- **If can optimize existing equipment** → avoid $120K-400K investment (8 ovens)
- **Software cost**: $12K/year (FlexiBake) vs $120K-400K capital expenditure
- **ROI of software**: 10-33× better than buying new equipment

**Key Finding**: Production scheduling software is **NOT about saving time** (though that's nice). It's about **maximizing equipment ROI** and **avoiding capital expenditure**.

### Platforms with Equipment Capacity Modeling
✅ **Advanced**:
- BatchMaster (enterprise-grade, complex capacity planning)

⚠️ **Basic**:
- FlexiBake (can schedule equipment, but no deep optimization)
- Katana (visual dashboard, basic resource management)
- MRPeasy (basic capacity planning, backward scheduling)

❌ **None**:
- BakeSmart, Craftybase, CaterZen, ClickUp

**Gap**: No mid-market platform ($100-500/mo) has advanced equipment capacity optimization. Either pay enterprise prices (BatchMaster $50K+/year) or use basic tools (FlexiBake, Katana) and manually optimize.

**Workaround**: Use FlexiBake/Katana for scheduling + custom spreadsheet for equipment optimization analysis.

## Recipe Management: Consistency vs Flexibility

### Wholesale Bakery (Consistency Critical)
- **Problem**: 3 shifts using different recipes for same product
- **Impact**: Quality variance, customer complaints, returns
- **Solution**: Centralized recipe database with version control

3. **Order ingredients**: Call/email suppliers (2-3 suppliers)

### Pain Point Details

#### 1. Multi-Day Production Sequencing
- **Example**: 150-person wedding (Friday evening)
  - Monday: Marinate proteins, make sauces
  - Tuesday: Prep vegetables, par-cook proteins
  - Wednesday: Bake desserts, prepare side dishes
  - Thursday: Final cooking, plating prep
  - Friday: Finishing touches, pack, deliver 2pm

- **Problem**: No systematic way to plan multi-day sequence
- **Current Method**: Head chef mentally plans or uses hand-written notes
- **Impact**: Forget steps, prep too early (quality degradation), prep too late (rush/stress)

#### 2. Equipment Conflicts
- **Problem**: 2 events in same week both need oven on Wednesday
- **Example**:
  - Event A (Saturday wedding, 120 people): Needs oven Wednesday for desserts
  - Event B (Thursday corporate lunch, 80 people): Needs oven Wednesday for proteins
  - Resolution: Move Event A desserts to Tuesday, but Tuesday is already packed with Event B prep

- **Current Method**: Trial-and-error, move prep days around until conflicts resolve
- **Impact**: Inefficient scheduling, staff overtime, equipment sits idle some days while over-utilized others

#### 3. Ingredient Ordering Timing
- **Problem**: Order ingredients Monday for Friday event, but client changes headcount Thursday (150 → 180 people)
- **Impact**: Rush supplier order (premium pricing or unavailable), substitute ingredients (quality compromise), over-buy for future use (cash flow tied up in inventory)
- **Cost**: Estimated $8K-12K/year in rush orders and over-buying

#### 4. Last-Minute Menu Changes
- **Problem**: Client changes menu 24-48 hours before event (replace chicken with salmon)
- **Impact**: Already ordered chicken (waste or freeze for future use), rush order salmon, re-plan production schedule mid-week
- **Frequency**: 20-30% of events have last-minute changes
- **Cost**: Estimated $5K-10K/year in wasted ingredients and rush orders

- **Square POS**: Retail storefront sales
- **Email/phone**: Wholesale order entry (no online portal)

### Weekly Production Planning Process (Current)
1. **Monday morning** (2 hours): Production manager reviews wholesale orders (email/phone), retail forecast, inventory levels
2. **Create production schedule**: Manually allocate SKUs to production shifts in Excel
3. **Check ingredient availability**: Cross-reference inventory spreadsheet (often out-of-date)
4. **Assign ovens/equipment**: Manually schedule oven slots (bread 6am-10am, pastries 10am-2pm, etc.)
5. **Print production tickets**: Hand-written orders distributed to shift leads
6. **Mid-week adjustments**: Rush orders, equipment breakdowns, ingredient shortages require re-planning (1-2 hours)
7. **Friday review**: Variance analysis (planned vs actual production) - rarely done due to time constraints

**Total Time Spent Planning**: 8-10 hours/week (production manager)

### Pain Point Details

#### 1. Equipment Bottlenecks
- **Problem**: 8 ovens fully utilized 6am-2pm (100% capacity), then 30% utilized 2pm-6pm
- **Root Cause**: No visibility into equipment schedule, production planned by product type not equipment utilization
- **Impact**: Could produce 25-30% more if ovens were scheduled optimally
- **Revenue Opportunity**: $625K-750K additional annual revenue at current margins

#### 2. Ingredient Shortage Discovery
- **Problem**: Discover missing ingredients 2-3 times/week after production has started
- **Root Cause**: Inventory spreadsheet updated weekly (not real-time), no ingredient availability check before scheduling production
- **Impact**: Production delays (1-2 hours), ingredient substitutions (quality issues), rush supplier orders (premium pricing)
- **Cost**: Estimated $15K-25K/year in rush orders and lost productivity

#### 3. Recipe Inconsistency
- **Problem**: Night shift uses different flour ratio than day shift for same bread recipe
- **Root Cause**: No centralized recipe database, bakers use personal notebooks
- **Impact**: Product quality variance, customer complaints, returns/credits
- **Cost**: Estimated $10K-20K/year in returns/credits

#### 4. Unknown Product Profitability
- **Problem**: Know revenue per SKU, but not true COGS (ingredient costs change, labor allocation unclear, overhead not allocated)
- **Root Cause**: No recipe costing system, labor not tracked by product
- **Impact**: May be losing money on some products without knowing
- **Risk**: Selling unprofitable products at volume


**Key Insight**: The **5-year TCO inflection point** occurs at $2M-5M revenue. Below this, SaaS always wins. Above this, open source or custom builds become competitive—but only with technical capacity.

## Technical Capacity Assessment

### Do You Have Technical Capacity for Open Source?

**Requirements for Odoo/ERPNext**:
1. **Sysadmin** (20-40 hours/month) - Server management, updates, backups, monitoring
2. **Developer** (10-20 hours/month) - Module configuration, custom reports, workflow automation
3. **Total**: 30-60 hours/month = 0.2-0.3 FTE = $3K-5K/month = $36K-60K/year

**When You Have Capacity**:
- ✅ Already employ sysadmin/developer (can add Odoo to their workload)
- ✅ Multi-location operation (amortize technical staff across 2+ facilities)
- ✅ IT budget `>$100K/year` (technical staff already planned)

**When You DON'T Have Capacity**:
- ❌ No technical staff (would need to hire)
- ❌ Single location, `<50` employees (can't justify technical staff)
- ❌ Outsourcing complexity (prefer vendor handles infrastructure)

### Break-Even Analysis: Hiring Tech Staff for Odoo

**Scenario**: 50-employee wholesale bakery ($2.5M revenue)

**Option A: FlexiBake** (No tech staff needed)
- Software: $12,420/year
- Support: Included
- **Total**: $12,420/year

**Option B: Odoo** (Must hire tech staff)
- Software: $3,000/year (hosting)
- Sysadmin: $60,000/year (shared with other systems)
- **Total**: $63,000/year

**Odoo LOSES at single location** (5× more expensive when include sysadmin)

**Scenario**: 2-location bakery ($5M revenue)

**Option A: FlexiBake** (2 instances)
- Software: $12,420 × 2 = $24,840/year

**Option B: Odoo** (1 sysadmin, 2 instances)
- Software: $3,000 × 2 = $6,000/year (hosting)
- Sysadmin: $60,000/year (shared across both locations)
- **Total**: $66,000/year

**Odoo still LOSES** (2.7× more expensive)

**Scenario**: 4-location bakery ($10M revenue)

**Option A: FlexiBake** (4 instances)
- Software: $12,420 × 4 = $49,680/year

**Option B: Odoo** (1 sysadmin, 4 instances)
- Software: $3,000 × 4 = $12,000/year
- Sysadmin: $60,000/year (shared across all locations)
- **Total**: $72,000/year

**Odoo still LOSES** (1.4× more expensive)

**Break-Even Point**: 6-8 locations OR when sysadmin is shared across OTHER systems (not just Odoo)

**Key Insight**: **Odoo only wins if you ALREADY HAVE technical staff** (for other IT systems) and can add Odoo to their workload. Hiring sysadmin JUST FOR Odoo rarely makes economic sense.

**When Odoo Makes Sense**:
- ✅ Already have IT department (sysadmin managing email, network, ERP) → add Odoo to workload
- ✅ 6+ locations (amortize sysadmin across many instances)
- ✅ Need heavy customization (SaaS platforms too rigid)
- ✅ Risk-averse about vendor lock-in (willing to pay premium for control)

## Data Portability Checklist

### Before Signing Contract

1. ✅ **Negotiate data portability clause**
   - Guarantee of full data export (all tables, all fields)
   - Export format (CSV, JSON, SQL dump)
   - Vendor assistance with export (included in contract)
   - Timeline for export (available within 30 days of request)

2. ✅ **Verify API availability**
   - Read access to all data (recipes, orders, inventory, production schedules)
   - Write access (test import/export workflows before committing)
   - API documentation (comprehensive, with examples)
   - Rate limits (sufficient for data migration)

3. ✅ **Review exit clause**
   - Notice period (30-90 days)
   - Data retention post-cancellation (60-90 days to export data)
   - No hostage clauses (can export data even if in arrears)

---

### During Implementation

1. ✅ **Maintain parallel systems** (first 3-6 months)
   - Keep spreadsheets active until confident in new platform
   - Export data monthly (test export process works)

2. ✅ **Create neutral recipe database**
   - Maintain recipes in Airtable or Google Sheets (in addition to platform)
   - If platform shuts down, recipes are safe
   - Update frequency: Quarterly (don't need real-time sync)

3. ✅ **Document custom workflows**
   - Screenshot configuration, custom fields, reports
   - If need to rebuild on new platform, documentation accelerates setup

---

### Quarterly Data Hygiene

1. ✅ **Export full data** (CSV or API)
   - Recipes, inventory, orders, production schedules, customer data
   - Store in cloud storage (Dropbox, Google Drive) separate from platform

2. ✅ **Test data restoration**
   - Import exported data into Excel or Airtable (verify completeness)
   - If can't restore data from export, export format is insufficient

3. ✅ **Monitor platform health**
   - Check for acquisition rumors (Crunchbase, industry news)
   - Review financial health (VC funding rounds, layoffs, product updates)
   - If see warning signs (slow support, no feature updates), accelerate exit planning
