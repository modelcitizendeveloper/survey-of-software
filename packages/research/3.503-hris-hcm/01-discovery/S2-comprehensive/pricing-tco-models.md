# S2 Comprehensive: Pricing & Total Cost of Ownership Models

**Last Updated**: October 31, 2025
**Analysis Type**: 3-year TCO across 8 platforms, 6 company size scenarios

---

## Executive Summary

Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) for HRIS/HCM platforms varies **10x** between the cheapest (Gusto at $72/employee/year) and most expensive (Oracle at $600/employee/year). The primary drivers are:

1. **Software subscription costs** (40-60% of TCO)
2. **Implementation fees** (20-40% of Year 1 costs for enterprise)
3. **Internal staffing** (HRIS team grows with platform complexity)
4. **Training & change management** (often underestimated)
5. **Ongoing support & maintenance** (quarterly updates, integration monitoring)

**Key Finding**: Platforms with **better UX cost less to operate** due to reduced training, support tickets, and HR admin time.

---

## TCO Calculation Methodology

### Cost Components

**Year 1 (Implementation Year)**:
1. Software subscription (12 months)
2. Implementation fees (one-time)
3. Data migration costs
4. Training & change management
5. Internal project team (FTE hours × loaded cost)
6. Lost productivity during transition

**Years 2-3 (Steady State)**:
1. Software subscription (annual increase 3-5%)
2. Ongoing support/managed services
3. Internal HRIS team (full-time staff)
4. Training for new hires
5. Platform updates/upgrades
6. Integration maintenance

**Excluded from TCO** (consistent across all platforms):
- Benefits broker fees
- 401k provider fees
- Background check costs
- Payroll tax filing (compliance costs)

### Scenario 1 Recommendation

**For 50-employee startup**:

1. **Choose Gusto** if:
   - Budget-conscious ($21K vs. $71K-$95K over 3 years)
   - Simple needs (payroll, benefits, basic HR)
   - Want flexibility (month-to-month)
   - Willing to add integrations later (TSheets for time, BambooHR for ATS)

2. **Choose BambooHR** if:
   - Hiring rapidly (need strong ATS)
   - Want performance management
   - HR team wants best-in-class HR tools
   - Can afford $50K more over 3 years

3. **Choose Rippling** if:
   - Tech company needing HR+IT unification
   - Global contractors (10+ countries)
   - Want maximum automation
   - Budget allows ($95K over 3 years)

**Bottom Line**: For most 50-employee startups, **Gusto** is the clear winner. Only choose BambooHR or Rippling if specific features (ATS, IT management) justify 3-4x cost premium.

## Hidden Costs & Gotchas

### 1. Implementation Cost Surprises

**Enterprise Implementations Often Run Over Budget**:
- **Workday**: Budget $2M, actual $2.5-3M (25-50% overruns common)
- **Oracle**: Budget $1.5M, actual $2-2.5M (33-67% overruns)

- Data quality issues (3-6 months cleaning legacy data not budgeted)

**Mitigation**:
- Add 30% contingency to implementation budget
- Lock scope in Phase 1 (defer enhancements to Phase 2 post-launch)

## ROI Calculations: When Premium Platforms Pay Off

### Paycom Beti® ROI (Employee-Driven Payroll)

**Scenario**: 500-employee company, HR team spends 40 hours/payroll on payroll processing

**Before Beti®** (Traditional Payroll):
- HR team reviews 500 paychecks manually
- Employees call HR with questions/corrections
- Payroll errors require off-cycle corrections
- **Total HR time**: 40 hours per payroll × 26 payrolls/year = 1,040 hours/year
- **Cost**: 1,040 hours × $75/hr loaded = $78,000/year

**After Beti®** (Employee-Driven):
- Employees review and approve own paychecks
- HR only handles exceptions (5% of 500 = 25 employees)
- Payroll errors drop 90% (employee verification catches mistakes)
- **Total HR time**: 4 hours per payroll × 26 payrolls = 104 hours/year
- **Cost**: 104 hours × $75/hr = $7,800/year

**Annual Savings**: $70,200/year

**Paycom Cost Premium vs. Rippling**:
- Paycom: $150,000/year (500 EE × $25/mo × 12)
- Rippling: $180,000/year (500 EE × $30/mo × 12)
- **Paycom is actually cheaper**, AND delivers $70K/year HR labor savings

**ROI**: Beti® pays for itself + saves money

### Rippling HR+IT Automation ROI

**Scenario**: 250-employee tech company, high employee turnover (25%/year)

**Before Rippling** (Manual Onboarding/Offboarding):
- New hire: HR provisions HRIS → emails IT → IT provisions laptop, Gmail, Slack, GitHub → takes 3 days
- Offboarding: HR sends termination email → IT manually disables 15 app accounts → takes 1 day, security risk (accounts not disabled immediately)
- **HR time**: 2 hours/hire + 1 hour/termination = 3 hours × 62 employees/year = 186 hours
- **IT time**: 4 hours/hire + 2 hours/termination = 6 hours × 62 = 372 hours

**After Rippling** (Automated Onboarding/Offboarding):
- New hire: HR clicks "Hire" → Rippling auto-provisions laptop, Gmail, Slack, GitHub, benefits in 1 workflow → takes 1 hour
- Offboarding: HR clicks "Terminate" → Rippling instantly disables all 15 app accounts → takes 15 minutes
- **HR time**: 1 hour/hire + 0.25 hour/termination = 1.25 hours × 62 = 77.5 hours
- **IT time**: 0 hours (fully automated)
- **Total cost**: 77.5 hours × $75/hr = $5,813/year

**Annual Savings**: $36,037/year

**Rippling Cost Premium vs. Gusto**:
- Rippling: $117,000/year (250 EE × $39/mo × 12)
- Gusto: $18,000/year (250 EE × $6/mo × 12 + $40 base)
- **Premium**: $99,000/year

**However**: Rippling also delivers:
- Global contractor payments (vs. Gusto US-only) → enables international hiring
- Device management (MDM for laptops) → replaces Jamf ($12/device/mo = $36K/year)
- Corporate cards (replaces Brex/Ramp) → saves $5K/year in card fees

**Total Rippling value**: $36K automation + $36K MDM + $5K cards = **$77K/year** → still $22K short of justifying $99K premium

**Conclusion**: Rippling ROI only works if you **need global contractors + IT management**. For pure HR, Gusto is better value.
