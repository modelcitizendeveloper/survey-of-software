## 3. AI & Skills Intelligence Evolution (2025-2030 Outlook)

### Current State (2025): Skills Cloud vs. Dynamic Skills

| Feature | Workday Skills Cloud | Oracle Dynamic Skills | Future Commoditization Risk |
|---------|---------------------|----------------------|----------------------------|
| **Skills Taxonomy Size** | 20,000+ skills | 10,000+ skills | **HIGH** (OpenAI, LinkedIn Graph will have 100K+ skills) |
| **AI Matching** (Candidate → Job) | Machine learning + graph database | Deep learning + NLP | **MEDIUM** (tech is maturing, but proprietary data moats remain) |
| **Career Pathing** | AI-recommended paths | AI-recommended paths | **HIGH** (algorithm becoming commoditized) |
| **Internal Talent Marketplace** | Yes (gig matching) | Yes (role matching) | **LOW** (workflow integration is defensible) |
| **Skills Gap Analysis** | Org-level + individual | Org-level + individual | **HIGH** (reporting feature, easily replicated) |
| **Pricing** | Included in Workday HCM | $3/EE/month add-on ($36K/year for 1K EE) | N/A |

**Key Insight**: Skills taxonomy and AI matching will commoditize by 2028-2030 as:
- **LinkedIn Skills Graph** (1B+ members, 40K+ skills) becomes public API
- **OpenAI / Anthropic LLMs** can generate skill recommendations from job descriptions
- **Open-source alternatives** (e.g., ESCO European skills taxonomy) gain traction

**Durable Moats** (Won't commoditize):
- **Integration with HRIS data**: Skills Cloud accessing 10 years of performance reviews, comp history, learning completions → proprietary training data
- **Talent marketplace workflows**: Not just "recommend a match" but "facilitate internal gig economy" (approvals, compensation, project tracking)
- **Actionable insights**: Moving beyond "Alice should learn Python" to "Assign Alice to Project X, enroll in Course Y, budget $5K"

---

### AI Evolution Roadmap (2025-2030)

**2025-2026: AI Assistants** (Current wave)
- **What**: Chatbots that answer employee questions
- **Examples**:
  - Paycom IWant™: "How much PTO do I have?" → AI answers from HRIS data
  - Workday Assistant: "Show my pay stubs for 2025" → AI retrieves, displays
- **Limitation**: Reactive (employee asks, AI answers)—not proactive

**2027-2028: AI Agents** (Next wave)
- **What**: Autonomous agents that complete multi-step tasks
- **Examples**:
  - **Benefits enrollment AI**: Agent analyzes employee health history (with consent) → recommends medical plan → enrolls employee → confirms with carrier
  - **Performance review AI**: Agent drafts performance review based on 1:1 notes, OKR completion, peer feedback → manager edits/approves
  - **Recruiting AI**: Agent screens resumes, schedules interviews, sends rejection emails (manager only reviews top 5 candidates)
- **Prediction**: By 2028, **50% of HR admin tasks automated by AI agents** (vs. 10% today via basic workflow automation)

**2029-2030: Predictive Workforce Intelligence** (Future wave)
- **What**: AI predicts workforce needs before humans recognize them
- **Examples**:
  - **Attrition prediction**: "Alice (Senior Engineer) has 85% flight risk—recommend $15K retention bonus or promotion to Staff Engineer"
  - **Skills gap forecasting**: "Company launching AI product in 18 months → need 20 ML engineers → start recruiting now or upskill 15 internal engineers?"
  - **Succession planning**: "Bob (VP Sales) retiring in 2 years → promote Carol (Director) → backfill Carol with Dave (Manager) → hire 2 new Account Executives"
- **Prediction**: By 2030, **HR becomes predictive** (vs. today's reactive/transactional)

---

### Who Will Win AI Race?

**Tier 1: Workday** (Best positioned)
- **Advantage**: 10+ years of HCM data (performance, compensation, learning) → proprietary AI training data
- **Skills Cloud head start**: 20,000+ skills, 11,000+ customers, network effects
- **Investment**: Workday AI Labs (200+ ML engineers, $500M/year R&D)
- **Moat**: Data moat (can't replicate without 10 years of customer data)

**Tier 2: Oracle, ADP** (Catching up)
- **Oracle**: Dynamic Skills launched 2020 (4-year lag behind Workday), catching up via aggressive investment
- **ADP**: ADP DataCloud (75 years of payroll data) → unique data asset for workforce analytics, but behind on skills/talent intelligence

**Tier 3: Mid-market** (Paycom, Paylocity, Rippling) (Will integrate 3rd-party AI)
- **Strategy**: Partner with OpenAI, Anthropic, or LinkedIn for AI capabilities (vs. building in-house)
- **Example**: Rippling partners with OpenAI for resume screening AI (launched 2025)
- **Risk**: No proprietary data moat → commoditized AI features

**Tier 4: SMB** (Gusto, BambooHR) (Basic AI only)
- **Reality**: SMB platforms serve 10-500 employees → AI ROI unclear (don't have scale for AI investment)
- **Future**: Will add basic chatbots (via OpenAI API integration), but not advanced AI agents

### Will Skills Cloud Become Commoditized?

**Yes, Partially** (2028-2030):
- **Commoditized**: Skills taxonomy, AI matching algorithms, career path recommendations
- **Reason**: LLMs (GPT-5, Claude 4) can generate these from public data (LinkedIn, O*NET, ESCO)
- **Impact**: Workday's $0 pricing (Skills Cloud included) vs. Oracle's $3/EE/month becomes irrelevant (both free eventually)

**No, Core Value Remains**:
- **Durable**: Internal talent marketplace workflows, succession planning, workforce forecasting
- **Reason**: Requires deep HRIS integration + proprietary company data (Workday's 10-year performance/comp history)
- **Moat**: New entrants can't replicate without existing customer base

**Prediction**: By 2030, **skills intelligence becomes table stakes** (all HRIS platforms have basic skills features), but **strategic workforce planning remains differentiator** (only Workday, Oracle have data depth for predictive analytics).

## 4. Build vs. Buy Analysis

### Could You Build HRIS In-House?

**TL;DR**: **Almost never worth it** (only for mega-tech companies with 100,000+ employees)

**Cost to Build** (Minimum Viable Product):
- **Engineering**: 10 engineers × 2 years × $200K/year = $4M
- **Product/Design**: 2 PM + 2 designers × 2 years × $150K = $1.2M
- **Infrastructure**: AWS/Azure hosting $200K/year × 2 years = $400K
- **Compliance**: Payroll tax engine licensing (50 states) = $500K/year
- **Total**: **$6.1M over 2 years** (just to build MVP)

**Ongoing Costs**:
- **Engineering**: 5 FTE × $200K = $1M/year (maintenance, new features)
- **Infrastructure**: $300K/year (scales with employees)
- **Compliance**: $500K/year (tax tables, labor law updates)
- **Total**: **$1.8M/year** ongoing

**What You Get** (Custom HRIS):
- Core HR (employee records, org chart)
- Basic payroll (US-only, 50-state tax compliance)
- Benefits administration (carrier integrations)
- Time & attendance (timecards, PTO)
- **Missing**: Recruiting, performance, learning, compensation planning, analytics, mobile app, global payroll, AI features

**Comparison** (1,000 employees):
- **Build**: $6.1M (Year 1-2) + $1.8M/year = **$9.7M over 5 years**
- **Buy Paycom**: $260K/year × 5 = **$1.3M over 5 years**
- **Buy Workday**: $150K/year × 5 + $600K implementation = **$1.35M over 5 years**

**Build is 7x more expensive than Buy** ($9.7M vs. $1.3M)

---

### When Build Might Make Sense

**Scenario 1: Mega-Tech Company** (100,000+ employees)
- **Example**: Google built internal HRIS (Workday-like system for 180,000 employees)
- **Rationale**:
  - Scale justifies investment ($1.8M/year ongoing ÷ 100K EE = **$18/EE/year** vs. Workday $150-200/EE/year)
  - Custom needs (Google-specific eng levels, unique comp structures)
  - Engineering talent available (can staff 10-engineer team from internal talent)
- **Break-even**: ~50,000 employees (build cost drops below Workday cost)

**Scenario 2: Vertical SaaS Company** (Building HR for specific industry)
- **Example**: Homebase (built time tracking + payroll for restaurants/retail)
- **Rationale**: Not building for internal use, but to **sell as product** (B2B SaaS business model)
- **Break-even**: Once 5,000+ customers using product (SaaS revenue exceeds build cost)

**Scenario 3: Government / Highly Regulated** (Custom compliance requirements)
- **Example**: Military HR systems (TS/SCI clearance requirements, unique pay tables)
- **Rationale**: Commercial HRIS can't meet security/compliance needs (classified data, FedRAMP High)
- **Reality**: Even government increasingly uses Workday GovCloud (FedRAMP certified) vs. building custom

---

### Build vs. Buy Decision Framework

**Build In-House** if:
- ✅ 50,000+ employees (scale justifies cost)
- ✅ Engineering team of 10+ available (not diverting from core product)
- ✅ Unique requirements that no COTS HRIS can meet
- ✅ Multi-decade commitment (can't abandon after 2 years)

**Buy COTS HRIS** if:
- ✅ <50,000 employees (build cost exceeds buy cost)
- ✅ Standard HR processes (hire, pay, review, promote—90% of companies)
- ✅ Want to focus engineering on core product (not HR tech)
- ✅ Need to go-live within 12 months (build takes 2+ years)

**For 99.9% of companies: Buy COTS HRIS** ✅
