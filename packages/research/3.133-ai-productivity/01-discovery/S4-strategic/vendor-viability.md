## User Impact of Acquisition

### Best Case: Product Continues (Google, Microsoft, Atlassian)
- Acquisition by Big Tech (Google, Microsoft) → product integrated into Calendar/Outlook
- **Impact**: Free for existing users (Google Workspace, Office 365), better integrations
- **Risk**: Product evolution slows (big-company bureaucracy)

**Example**: Google acquires Reclaim → Reclaim features in Google Calendar (free for Workspace)

---

### Moderate Case: Product Transitions (Atlassian, Notion)
- Acquisition by mid-size player (Atlassian, Notion) → product rebranded
- **Impact**: Existing users migrated to acquirer's platform (may require re-setup)
- **Risk**: Integration changes (e.g., Motion → Atlassian, must use Jira)

**Example**: Atlassian acquires Motion → Motion features integrated into Jira (existing users migrate)

---

### Worst Case: Product Discontinued (Acqui-Hire)
- Acqui-hire (team hired, product shut down)
- **Impact**: Users must migrate to competitor (2-4 hours, data export)
- **Risk**: Lost AI training data (2-4 weeks recalibration on new platform)

**Example**: Motion acqui-hired by Microsoft → product discontinued, users migrate to Reclaim/Todoist

## Mitigation Strategies

### 1. Choose Low Lock-In Providers
- **Reclaim**: Tasks stay in Todoist/Asana (no lock-in)
- **Todoist**: Established player (10+ years, low acquisition risk)
- **Avoid**: Motion (all-in-one, higher lock-in if vendor dies)

### 2. Export Data Regularly
- Motion: Export tasks to CSV (quarterly backup)
- Reclaim: Tasks already in Todoist/Asana (no backup needed)
- **Cost**: 15 min/quarter (low effort)

### 3. Have Migration Plan
- Identify backup tool (if Motion dies → switch to Reclaim)
- Test migration (export CSV, import to backup tool)
- **Effort**: 1-2 hours (one-time planning)

### 4. Diversify Tools (Avoid All-in-One)
- Use Reclaim (calendar optimization) + Todoist (task management)
- If Reclaim dies → keep Todoist, switch to Motion
- **Trade-off**: Two tools vs one, but lower lock-in
