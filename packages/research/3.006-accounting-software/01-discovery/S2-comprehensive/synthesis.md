## Key Decision Frameworks

### Framework 1: Free vs Paid ($0-$100K revenue)
**Choose Wave Free when:**
- Revenue <$50K
- Simple invoicing + expense tracking sufficient
- No inventory needs
- Manual processes acceptable

**Upgrade to paid when:**
- Revenue >$100K
- Need automation (bank feeds, categorization)
- Need inventory tracking
- Accountant requires QuickBooks/Xero

---

### Framework 2: Small Business Choice ($100K-$5M)
**Choose QuickBooks when:**
- US-based business
- Need inventory management
- Accountant preference (most know QuickBooks)
- Want largest app ecosystem

**Choose Xero when:**
- International operations
- Need unlimited users (huge value)
- Want modern UX and mobile
- Growing team (5-25 users)

**Choose FreshBooks when:**
- Service business (consulting, agency, law)
- Billing by time/project
- Time tracking is critical
- Don't need inventory

**Choose Zoho Books when:**
- Budget-conscious
- Already using Zoho ecosystem
- International presence
- Need inventory at lower price

---

### Framework 3: Mid-Market Graduation ($5M-$100M)
**Choose Sage Intacct when:**
- Accounting is 80%+ of software need
- Multi-entity consolidation (2-10 entities)
- Professional services or SaaS company
- Advanced financial reporting critical
- Don't need manufacturing/supply chain

**Choose NetSuite when:**
- Need full ERP (accounting <50% of use case)
- E-commerce is core business
- Manufacturing or distribution company
- Complex multi-subsidiary (5+ entities)
- Revenue $10M-$1B range

**Choose Dynamics 365 when:**
- Heavily invested in Microsoft stack
- Office 365, Azure, Power Platform users
- Need ERP + Microsoft ecosystem benefits
- Revenue $5M-$200M range

## Feature Gaps & Trade-offs

### Wave
✅ Free, good for micro businesses
❌ No inventory, limited automation (free), no multi-entity

### Zoho Books
✅ Budget-friendly, Zoho ecosystem integration
❌ Smaller non-Zoho ecosystem, tier-locked features

### FreshBooks
✅ Best time tracking, service business focus
❌ No inventory, limited financial depth

### QuickBooks Online
✅ Most comprehensive small business features
❌ Price creep, complexity, frequent upselling

### Xero
✅ Unlimited users, modern UX, international
❌ Inventory less robust than QuickBooks

### Sage Intacct
✅ Best mid-market accounting, consolidation
❌ Expensive for small business, not full ERP

### NetSuite
✅ Comprehensive ERP, scales to $1B+
❌ Expensive, complex, hard to exit

### Dynamics 365
✅ Microsoft ecosystem, Power Platform
❌ Microsoft lock-in, accounting depth vs Sage Intacct

## Strategic Recommendations

### Start Small, Graduate Strategically
**Recommended path:**
1. **$0-$500K:** Wave or QuickBooks/Xero
2. **$500K-$5M:** QuickBooks Plus or Xero Established
3. **$5M-$100M:** Sage Intacct (accounting) or NetSuite (ERP)
4. **$100M+:** NetSuite, SAP, Oracle ERP

### Avoid Common Mistakes
1. **Don't stay in Wave too long** (revenue >$100K)
2. **Don't jump to ERP too early** (Sage Intacct better value $5M-$25M)
3. **Don't ignore accountant preference** (QuickBooks dominance is real)
4. **Don't forget TCO** (implementation + training + add-ons)
5. **Don't underestimate migration pain** (especially from NetSuite)

### Integration Strategy
- **QuickBooks users:** Leverage largest small business ecosystem
- **Xero users:** Best global integrations, excellent API
- **Zoho users:** Stay in Zoho ecosystem for tight integration
- **Microsoft users:** Dynamics 365 + Power Platform is powerful
- **Salesforce users:** Sage Intacct has native connector
