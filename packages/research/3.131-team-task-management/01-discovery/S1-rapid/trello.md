**Research Date**: November 12, 2025
**Profile Depth**: Full

## 1. Company Overview

**Founded**: 2011
**Founders**: Joel Spolsky, Michael Pryor (Fog Creek Software)
**Status**: Owned by Atlassian (acquired 2017 for $425M)
**Parent Revenue**: Atlassian $4.4B revenue (2024)
**Headquarters**: New York, NY (now Sydney, Australia under Atlassian)
**Employees**: Part of Atlassian (~12,000 total)

### Market Positioning

"Trello helps teams move work forward."

Trello positions itself as the **simple, visual kanban** tool - boards, lists, cards. That's it. No complexity, no learning curve. The anti-ClickUp.

### Core Value Proposition

- **Simplicity**: 5-minute learning curve (literally)
- **Visual kanban**: Boards make work tangible
- **Free forever**: Generous free tier, low-cost paid plans
- **Universal**: Anyone can use it (engineers, marketers, families)

## 2. Core Features

### Kanban Boards (Core Concept)
- **Boards**: Projects or workflows
- **Lists**: Columns (To Do, Doing, Done)
- **Cards**: Individual tasks
- **Drag & drop**: Move cards between lists
- **That's it**: Simple by design

### Card Features
- **Title & description**: Basic task info
- **Members**: Assign people to cards
- **Labels**: Color-coded categories
- **Due dates**: Deadlines
- **Checklists**: Sub-items within cards
- **Attachments**: Files, images, links
- **Comments**: Discussions per card
- **Activity log**: See card history

### Views (Limited)
- **Board view**: Default kanban (only view on Free)
- **Table view**: Spreadsheet-like (Premium+)
- **Timeline view**: Gantt-style (Premium+)
- **Calendar view**: Due dates (Premium+)
- **Dashboard view**: Metrics (Premium+)
- **Map view**: Geo-location (Premium+)

**Note**: Only 1 view on Free plan, 6 views total (fewest of all 6 platforms)

### Power-Ups (Integrations & Extensions)
- **Power-Ups**: Trello's term for add-ons
- **1 Power-Up on Free plan**
- **Unlimited on paid plans**
- **Examples**: Slack, Google Drive, Calendar, Voting, Custom Fields

### Automation (Butler)
- **Butler**: No-code automation tool
- **Unlimited automations** on paid plans (250 runs/month on Free)
- **Rules**: "When card moved to Done, archive it"
- **Buttons**: Custom actions
- **Card buttons**: Actions on specific cards
- **Board buttons**: Actions across board

### Collaboration Features
- **Comments & mentions**
- **Activity feed**: See all changes
- **Voting**: Team vote on cards (via Power-Up)
- **Card aging**: Dim old cards (visual indicator)

## 3. Team Collaboration

### Permission Models

| Role | Permissions |
|------|-------------|
| **Observer** | View-only (read cards, can't edit) |
| **Member** | Add/edit cards, comment |
| **Admin** | Manage board settings, members |
| **Workspace Admin** | Manage entire workspace, billing |

### Board-Level Visibility
- **Private boards**: Invitation only
- **Workspace boards**: Visible to all workspace members
- **Public boards**: Anyone with link can view (shareable)

### Real-Time Collaboration
- Live updates (see changes instantly)
- Activity notifications
- Mobile push notifications
- Email digests

## 4. Pricing Structure (2025)

### Free Plan
- **Cost**: $0
- **User limit**: Unlimited (up to 10 collaborators per board)
- **Boards**: Unlimited
- **Features**:
  - Unlimited cards
  - Unlimited storage (250MB per file attachment)
  - 1 Power-Up per board
  - Unlimited activity log
  - iOS & Android apps
  - 2-factor authentication
  - 250 automated command runs/month (Butler)
- **Limitations**:
  - Only board view (no calendar, timeline, table)
  - Only 1 Power-Up per board
  - No advanced checklists
  - No priority support

### Standard Plan
- **Cost**: $5/user/month (annual) or $6/user/month (monthly)
- **Features**:
  - Everything in Free, plus:
  - Unlimited Power-Ups
  - Advanced checklists
  - Custom fields
  - 1,000 automated command runs/month
  - Saved searches
  - Collections (organize boards)
- **Best for**: Small teams (3-15 people) needing integrations

### Premium Plan
- **Cost**: $10/user/month (annual) or $12.50/user/month (monthly)
- **Features**:
  - Everything in Standard, plus:
  - All views (Calendar, Timeline, Dashboard, Table, Map)
  - Workspace views (see across all boards)
  - Atlassian Intelligence AI
  - Admin & security features
  - Unlimited automated command runs
  - Priority support
  - Simple data export
- **Best for**: Growing teams (15-50 people) needing multiple views & AI

### Enterprise Plan
- **Cost**: $17.50/user/month (annual, decreases with volume)
- **Minimum**: 50 users
- **Features**:
  - Everything in Premium, plus:
  - Organization-wide permissions
  - Attachment permissions
  - Public board management
  - Multi-board guests
  - Free workspaces
  - Power-Up administration
  - 99.9% uptime SLA
- **Best for**: Large organizations (50+ people) with security/compliance needs

### Cost Examples

| Team Size | Plan | Monthly Cost (Annual) | Annual Cost |
|-----------|------|----------------------|-------------|
| Free | Free | $0 | $0 |
| 5 users | Standard | $25 | $300 |
| 10 users | Standard | $50 | $600 |
| 20 users | Premium | $200 | $2,400 |
| 50 users | Enterprise | $875 | $10,500 |

**Note**: Trello is the **cheapest** of all 6 platforms at $5/user (Standard), 29% cheaper than ClickUp ($7), 55% cheaper than Asana ($11)

## 5. Integration Ecosystem

### Power-Ups (Trello Integrations)

**Top Power-Ups**:
- **Slack**: Notifications, attach cards
- **Google Drive**: Attach files from Drive
- **Calendar**: Sync cards to Google/Outlook Calendar

### API Capabilities
- **REST API**: Full programmatic access
- **Webhooks**: Real-time events
- **Rate limits**: 300 requests/10 seconds per token (generous)
- **Documentation**: Good, comprehensive
- **SDKs**: Community JavaScript, Python, Ruby

### Zapier & Automation Platforms
- **Zapier**: 5,000+ app connections
- **Make (Integromat)**: Workflow automation
- **IFTTT**: Simple automation

### Developer Ecosystem
- Strong community (open since 2011)
- Many community-built Power-Ups
- Atlassian marketplace integration

## 6. AI Capabilities (2025)

### Atlassian Intelligence (Premium Plan)

**New Features (February 2025)**:
- **AI Inbox**: Capture tasks from any interaction, add to Trello
- **AI task suggestions**: "You mentioned this in Slack - create a card?"
- **Trello Planner**: Connect Google Calendar (Outlook coming soon)
- **Auto-scheduling**: AI suggests when to work on tasks

**Current AI Features**:
- **Smart suggestions**: AI recommends labels, due dates, assignees
- **Auto-categorize**: Sort cards into lists using AI
- **Priority scoring**: AI suggests card priority based on description

### Limitations
- **Only on Premium plan** ($10/user)
- **Very new** (February 2025 launch)
- **Limited compared to competitors**: No AI writing, summaries, or project briefs
- **Focused on task capture**: Inbox management, not complex workflows

## 8. Ideal Customer Profile

### Team Size Sweet Spot

**Best fit**: 1-15 people
- Individuals: Free plan perfect for personal use
- Small teams (3-10): Standard plan sufficient
- Medium teams (10-15): Premium plan adds views & AI

**Can work**: 15-50 people
- Premium plan scales to ~50 people
- But complexity becomes an issue
- May need enterprise tools beyond this

**Not ideal**: 50+ people or complex projects
- Enterprise plan exists, but competitors better
- Can't manage multiple teams, programs, portfolios
- Lacks features for large-scale coordination

### Industry / Use Case Fit

**Best for**:
1. **Simple projects**: Task lists, checklists, small initiatives
2. **Content/editorial calendars**: Blog posts, social media, campaigns
3. **Personal productivity**: To-do lists, goal tracking, habit formation
4. **Event planning**: Simple logistics, checklists
5. **Small team coordination**: Startups, freelancers, agencies (simple workflows)
6. **Onboarding**: New hires, training checklists
7. **Sales pipelines** (basic): Lead tracking, follow-ups

**Not ideal for**:
1. **Complex software development**: Linear/Jira better (dependencies, sprints)
2. **Enterprise portfolio management**: Asana/Monday/Jira better
3. **Resource planning**: No workload/capacity features
4. **Data-heavy workflows**: Airtable better (relational data)
5. **Advanced reporting needs**: Dashboards too basic

### Technical vs Non-Technical Users

**Non-technical friendly**: ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê (5/5)
- Easiest tool of all 6 platforms
- 5-minute learning curve
- No technical skills required
- Universally understood (board, list, card)
- Training time: <10 minutes

**Technical user perspective**:
- Developers often find it too simple
- Prefer Linear/Jira for code workflows
- But use Trello for personal tasks (simplicity appreciated)
- API is solid for custom integrations

### Key Decision Criteria

Choose Trello if you value:
1. **Simplicity** (5-minute learning curve)
2. **Free plan** (unlimited users, boards, cards)
3. **Low cost** ($5/user cheapest of all)
4. **Visual kanban** (drag-and-drop satisfying)
5. **Universal tool** (anyone can use it)

Skip Trello if you need:
1. **Complex workflows** (dependencies, advanced Gantt)
2. **Advanced reporting** (dashboards, analytics)
3. **Resource planning** (workload, capacity)
4. **Multiple views on free plan** (only board view free)
5. **Scalability** (large teams, complex projects)

## 9. Competitive Positioning

### How Trello Positions Against Competitors

**vs Asana**: "We're simple and free. Asana is complex and expensive."
- Trello: Simple, visual, cheap
- Asana: Powerful, structured, costly

**vs ClickUp**: "We're focused and easy. ClickUp is overwhelming and confusing."
- Trello: One thing (kanban), done well
- ClickUp: Everything, but complex

**vs Monday.com**: "We're affordable and straightforward. Monday is pretty but pricey."
- Trello: $5/user, simple
- Monday: $12/user, visual but expensive

**vs Linear**: "We're for everyone. Linear is only for engineers."
- Trello: Universal, non-technical
- Linear: Developer-focused, keyboard-driven

**vs Jira**: "We're simple kanban. Jira is enterprise complexity."
- Trello: Simple, fast setup
- Jira: Powerful, but takes weeks to learn

### Unique Selling Propositions (USPs)

1. **Simplest tool on market**:
   - 5-minute learning curve
   - No training required
   - Anyone can use it

2. **Best free plan**:
   - Unlimited users, boards, cards
   - Only limitation: 1 Power-Up per board
   - No time limits, no forced upgrades

3. **Lowest cost**:
   - $5/user Standard plan (cheapest)
   - $10/user Premium (cheapest for multi-view)

4. **Universal kanban**:
   - Works for any use case
   - Not opinionated (Linear, Jira are)
   - From personal to-do to team projects

### Market Share / Adoption Signals

- **Active users**: 50M+ users globally
- **Paying customers**: Unknown (Atlassian doesn't disclose)
- **Notable companies**: Pixar, Google, Fender, Kickstarter
- **G2 rating**: 4.4/5 (13,400+ reviews)
- **Capterra rating**: 4.5/5 (23,600+ reviews - highest review count)

**Market position**: Most widely used tool (50M users), but many on free plan. #1 in simplicity, #1 in adoption, but not #1 in revenue.

### Why Teams Choose Trello (Top 3 Reasons)

1. **Simplicity** (easiest to learn and use)
2. **Free plan** (generous, unlimited)
3. **Visual kanban** (satisfying drag-and-drop)

### Why Teams Leave Trello (Top 3 Reasons)

1. **Outgrew it** (need more features)
2. **Limited reporting** (can't show progress to stakeholders)
3. **Not scalable** (too many cards = messy)
