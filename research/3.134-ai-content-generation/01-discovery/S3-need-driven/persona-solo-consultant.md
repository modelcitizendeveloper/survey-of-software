# Persona: Solo Consultant / Fractional Executive

**Research Phase**: S3 Need-Driven Discovery (MPSE v3.0)
**Date**: November 2025

---

## Persona Profile

### Demographics
- **Role**: Independent consultant, fractional executive, or professional services provider
- **Experience**: 10-25 years in their domain (strategy, operations, finance, technology, etc.)
- **Income**: $100-300/hour billable rate, $150-400K annual revenue
- **Team Size**: Solo (may have VA or part-time assistant)
- **Tech Savviness**: Moderate (comfortable with SaaS tools, not a developer)

### Pain Points
- **Time Scarcity**: Every hour on content = one hour NOT billable to clients
- **Visibility Paradox**: Need active social presence to attract clients, but client work takes all time
- **Inconsistent Posting**: Go weeks without content during busy client periods
- **Writing Fatigue**: After writing proposals, reports, and client docs all day, don't want to write social posts
- **Perceived Judgment**: "Will clients think I'm not busy if I post too much?"

### Current Content Workflow (Pre-AI)
1. Think about posting something (10 minutes, happens irregularly)
2. Draft LinkedIn post or Twitter thread (30-45 minutes)
3. Abandon because clients need attention OR
4. Quickly publish without editing, regret quality
5. **Result**: 1-3 posts per month instead of 3-5 per week goal

**Time Investment**: 2-4 hours/month (inconsistent)
**Satisfaction Level**: 3/10 (frustrated by inconsistency)

## Typical Content Needs

### Priority 1: Social Media Thought Leadership
- **LinkedIn Posts**: 3-5 per week (primary platform)
- **Twitter/X Threads**: 2-3 per week (optional, depends on industry)
- **Content Types**:
  - Client success stories (anonymized)
  - Industry insights and trends
  - Contrarian takes or hot takes
  - "Lessons learned from [recent project]"
  - Framework or methodology explanations
  - Commentary on news/events in industry

**Volume**: 15-20 social posts per month
**Current Output**: 2-4 posts per month (gap: 75% below target)

### Priority 2: Case Studies / Blog Content
- **Frequency**: 1-2 long-form pieces per quarter (not monthly)
- **Purpose**:
  - Demonstrate expertise to prospective clients
  - Provide "deep dive" content for email newsletters
  - SEO for website (secondary concern - most leads from referrals/social)
- **Length**: 1,500-2,500 words
- **Topics**:
  - Detailed case studies with results
  - Comprehensive guides or frameworks
  - Industry trend analysis

**Volume**: 4-8 blog posts per year
**Current Output**: 2-4 posts per year (gap: 50% below target)

### Priority 3: Client Communication
- **Proposals**: 2-5 per month (must be customized, high stakes)
- **Status Updates**: Weekly emails to active clients
- **Follow-Up**: Post-project wrap-up emails and case study requests

**Note**: AI can assist here, but personalization is critical (can't sound generic)

### Priority 4: Email Marketing
- **Newsletter**: Monthly or quarterly (aspirational for most)
- **Current Reality**: Many consultants have email list but rarely send
- **Opportunity**: Repurpose blog content into newsletter sections

## Tool Selection Criteria

### Must-Haves
1. **Easy to use**: Can't spend 4 hours learning a complex tool
2. **Quick results**: Need to generate content in 5-10 minutes
3. **Professional quality**: Output must not sound "AI-generated"
4. **Flexible**: Works for LinkedIn, Twitter, emails, proposals
5. **Free trial or tier**: Must validate before paying

### Nice-to-Haves
1. **Brand voice training**: Learns consultant's writing style
2. **Templates**: Pre-built frameworks for common content types
3. **Mobile access**: Generate content on-the-go
4. **Integration**: Works with LinkedIn, email, scheduling tools
5. **Multi-language**: For consultants with international clients (rare need)

### Deal-Breakers
1. **Complex setup**: If takes more than 30 minutes to start using, won't adopt
2. **Generic output**: If sounds like every other AI post, defeats purpose
3. **No free trial**: Won't pay without testing first
4. **Per-word limits on paid tiers**: Causes anxiety about usage

3. **Generate first batch** (Week 1):
   - 10 LinkedIn posts on different topics

   - 2 email newsletter snippets
4. **Test quality** (Week 1-2):

   - Monitor engagement vs manual posts

5. **Refine workflow** (Week 3-4):
   - Improve prompts based on results

**Success Metrics**:

- Consistency: Able to post 3x per week

## Common Objections and Solutions

### Objection 1: "Will clients think I'm using AI?"

**Reality**: Clients care about YOUR expertise, not how you communicate it
- AI is a tool (like spell-check or Grammarly)
- Final content reflects your knowledge and insights
- 5-10 minutes of editing adds personal touch

**Solution**:
- Add personal anecdotes or examples
- Edit for voice and authenticity
- Use AI for structure/drafts, not final copy

**Industry Data**: 60% of professionals already use AI for content creation (2025)

### Objection 2: "I don't have time to learn a new tool"

**Reality**: 10-minute learning curve for Copy.ai or Rytr

**Solution**:
- Start with 1 template (LinkedIn post)
- Generate 3-5 posts in first session
- Learn by doing (not by reading manuals)

**Time Investment**: 30 minutes to get productive, 2 hours to become proficient

### Objection 3: "My clients expect original thinking"

**Reality**: AI generates structure, YOU provide expertise and insights

**Solution**:
- Use AI for first draft (saves 70% of time)
- Add your analysis, examples, and conclusions (30% effort)
- Result: Original thinking, less writing fatigue

**Example**: AI writes "5 Reasons [Topic]", you edit with YOUR specific insights and client examples

### Objection 4: "What if my content sounds generic?"

**Reality**: Generic output = generic input (poor prompts)

**Solution**:
- Train brand voice with sample content
- Provide specific prompts (not "write a post about strategy")
- Edit for unique perspective and voice

**Quality Control**: 5-10 minute review ensures authenticity

### Objection 5: "I can't afford another subscription"

**Reality**: Free tier is sufficient for many consultants

**Solution**:
- Start with Copy.ai Free (2,000 words = 20-30 posts)
- Stay on free tier if volume stays low
- Upgrade only if exceeding limits

**Alternative**: Rytr Free (10,000 chars) or ChatGPT Free (unlimited)
