# Airtable: Platform Profile

**Research Date**: November 12, 2025
**Profile Depth**: Full

---

## 1. Company Overview

**Founded**: 2012
**Founders**: Howie Liu (CEO), Andrew Ofstad, Emmett Nicholas
**Status**: Private
**Valuation**: $11B (2021) - highest valuation of all 6 platforms
**Headquarters**: San Francisco, CA
**Employees**: ~1,000

### Market Positioning

"A low-code platform to build next-generation apps."

Airtable positions itself as the **spreadsheet-database hybrid** - the flexibility of Excel with the power of a database. Not purely a PM tool, but a platform to build custom workflows, CRMs, and databases that happen to include task management.

### Core Value Proposition

- **Flexibility**: Build anything (CRM, project tracker, inventory system)
- **Database power**: Relational data, linked records, complex queries
- **Spreadsheet familiar**: Looks like Excel, easier than coding
- **Customization**: Adapt to unique business needs

## 2. Core Features

### Database Tables (Not Just "Tasks")
- **Tables**: Core unit (like database tables or Excel sheets)
- **Records**: Rows (like tasks, but more structured)
- **Fields**: Columns (30+ field types)
- **Linked records**: Relationships between tables (foreign keys)
- **Views**: Multiple ways to visualize same data

### Field Types (30+ Types)
- **Single line text**: Short text
- **Long text**: Paragraphs, descriptions
- **Attachments**: Files, images
- **Checkbox**: Boolean values
- **Multiple select**: Tags, categories
- **Single select**: Dropdown
- **Date**: Due dates, timestamps
- **Phone number**: Formatted phone
- **Email**: Validated email addresses
- **URL**: Links
- **Number**: Integers, decimals
- **Currency**: Money values
- **Percent**: Percentage values
- **Rating**: Star ratings
- **Formula**: Calculate values
- **Rollup**: Aggregate linked records
- **Count**: Count related records
- **Lookup**: Pull data from linked records
- **And 12+ more...**

### Multiple Views (6 View Types)
- **Grid view**: Spreadsheet-like table (default)
- **Form view**: Collect data via forms
- **Calendar view**: Timeline by date field
- **Gallery view**: Cards with images
- **Kanban view**: Drag-and-drop board
- **Timeline view**: Gantt-style (paid plans only)

**Note**: Fewer views than Monday.com (15) or ClickUp (15), but views are data-driven

### Automation
- **Automations**: Trigger-based rules
- **100-25,000 runs/month** depending on plan
- **Integrations**: Connect to external apps
- **Scripts**: JavaScript for advanced logic

### Collaboration Features
- **Comments**: Per record or cell
- **@mentions**: Tag team members
- **Activity history**: See all changes
- **Record coloring**: Visual categorization
- **Proofing**: Mark up images/files with comments

## 4. Pricing Structure (2025)

### Free Plan
- **Cost**: $0
- **User limit**: Unlimited editors (!)
- **Record limit**: 1,000 records per base
- **Features**:
  - Unlimited bases
  - 1GB storage per base
  - Interface Designer
  - Extensions
  - Mobile apps
  - 100 automation runs/month
  - Form views
  - Grid, Calendar, Gallery, Kanban views
- **Limitations**:
  - No Timeline (Gantt) view
  - No advanced permissions
  - Limited automation runs
  - Limited integrations

### Team Plan
- **Cost**: $20/user/month (annual) or $24/user/month (monthly)
- **Minimum**: No minimum (can buy 1 seat)
- **Features**:
  - Everything in Free, plus:
  - 50,000 records per base
  - 20GB storage per base
  - 25,000 automation runs/month
  - Timeline view (Gantt)
  - Standard sync integrations
  - Extensions
  - 6-month revision history
  - **500 AI credits/month per user**
- **Best for**: Small teams (3-20 people) needing more records and storage

### Business Plan
- **Cost**: $54/user/month (annual) or $60/user/month (monthly)
- **Features**:
  - Everything in Team, plus:
  - 125,000 records per base
  - 100GB storage per base
  - Admin panel
  - Advanced extensions
  - 2-way sync integrations
  - 3-year revision history
  - **Unlimited AI credits**
- **Best for**: Growing teams (20-100 people) needing advanced features

### Enterprise Scale
- **Cost**: Custom pricing (contact sales)
- **Features**:
  - Everything in Business, plus:
  - 500,000 records per base
  - 1TB storage per base
  - SAML-based SSO
  - Advanced security & governance
  - Dedicated support
  - Onboarding & training
  - SLA guarantees
- **Best for**: Large organizations (100+ people) with security/compliance needs

### AI Add-On
- **Cost**: $6/user/month (on top of base plan)
- **Features**:
  - AI formula generation
  - AI automation enhancement
  - Natural language to formula

### Cost Examples

| Team Size | Plan | Monthly Cost (Annual) | Annual Cost |
|-----------|------|----------------------|-------------|
| 5 users | Team | $100 | $1,200 |
| 10 users | Team | $200 | $2,400 |
| 20 users | Business | $1,080 | $12,960 |
| 50 users | Business | $2,700 | $32,400 |

**Note**: Airtable is **82% more expensive** than Asana ($20 vs $11/user) and **186% more expensive** than ClickUp ($20 vs $7/user). Most expensive of all 6 platforms.

- **Limited AI compared to competitors**: No AI writing, no AI summaries
- **Focused on database operations**: Not general task management AI

## 8. Ideal Customer Profile

### Team Size Sweet Spot

**Best fit**: 5-30 people
- Large enough to justify cost
- Small enough to manage data complexity
- Teams with unique workflows (not standard PM)

**Can work**: 3-100 people
- 3-5 people: Expensive per user, but powerful
- 30-100 people: Works if budget allows
- 100+ people: Enterprise plan available

**Not ideal**: 1-2 people or cost-sensitive teams
- 1-2 people: $20/user too expensive for solo/tiny teams
- Budget-conscious: ClickUp $7, Asana $11 much cheaper

### Industry / Use Case Fit

**Best for**:
1. **Teams with structured data needs**: Inventory, CRM, databases
2. **Creative agencies**: Track clients, projects, assets, invoices in linked tables
3. **Marketing teams**: Campaign tracking, content calendar with rich data
4. **Operations teams**: Process management with custom data structures
5. **Product teams**: Feature planning with linked tables (customers, features, releases)
6. **Teams that outgrew spreadsheets**: Need database power, not ready for full dev work

**Not ideal for**:
1. **Traditional PM**: Asana/Monday better for standard task management
2. **Software development**: Linear/Jira better for code-centric workflows
3. **Simple projects**: Trello sufficient (Airtable overkill)
4. **Budget-conscious teams**: Too expensive ($20/user)
5. **Non-technical teams needing fast start**: Setup time too long

### Technical vs Non-Technical Users

**Non-technical friendly**: ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚òÜ‚òÜ (3/5)
- Looks like Excel (familiar)
- But requires relational thinking
- Can be overwhelming initially
- Training time: 1-2 weeks

**Technical user perspective**:
- Developers love the database model
- API excellent for custom integrations
- But may prefer coding custom solution (Airtable expensive)
- Good middle ground between spreadsheets and custom DB

### Key Decision Criteria

Choose Airtable if you value:
1. **Flexibility** (build custom workflows, CRM, databases)
2. **Relational data** (linked tables, rollups, lookups)
3. **Data management** (need database power, not just tasks)
4. **Unique workflows** (standard PM tools don't fit)
5. **No-code platform** (build custom apps without coding)

Skip Airtable if you need:
1. **Traditional PM** (Asana/Monday better)
2. **Low cost** (ClickUp $7, Asana $11 much cheaper)
3. **Fast setup** (Asana/Trello ready instantly)
4. **Advanced Gantt/dependencies** (MS Project, Smartsheet better)
5. **Simple task lists** (Airtable is overkill)

## 9. Competitive Positioning

### How Airtable Positions Against Competitors

**vs Excel**: "We're Excel on steroids - relational data, collaboration, automation, apps."
- Airtable: Database power, real-time collaboration
- Excel: Spreadsheet, local files, limited collaboration

**vs Asana/Monday**: "We're a platform to build custom workflows. They're opinionated PM tools."
- Airtable: Flexible, database-first, build anything
- Asana/Monday: Opinionated, task-first, PM-focused

**vs Notion**: "We're for structured data. Notion is for documents and wikis."
- Airtable: Database, structured records
- Notion: Documents, knowledge management

**vs Salesforce**: "We're a no-code CRM you can customize without developers."
- Airtable: No-code, affordable, flexible
- Salesforce: Enterprise, complex, expensive

### Unique Selling Propositions (USPs)

1. **Spreadsheet-database hybrid**:
   - Looks like Excel

   - Unique positioning

2. **Extreme flexibility**:

   - Not limited to one use case

3. **Relational data power**:
   - Linked records (foreign keys)
   - Rollup & lookup fields

   - No competitor offers this

4. **No-code platform**:
   - Build custom apps without coding
