## Free Tier Analysis

### Most Generous Free Tiers

**1. PostHog**
- 1M events/month FREE forever
- 15K session replays/month FREE
- 1M feature flag requests/month FREE
- All core features included (no feature gating)
- Unlimited team members
- **Best for**: Startups growing from 0 to scale

**2. Mixpanel**
- 1M events/month FREE (recently reduced from 20M)
- Unlimited users
- 1-year data retention
- **Limitations**: Only 5 saved reports, no advanced features
- **Best for**: Early validation and simple analytics

**3. Amplitude**
- 100K Monthly Tracked Users (MTUs) FREE
- Can equal millions of events depending on user base
- Unlimited chart saves
- 1-year data retention
- **Best for**: Apps with lower unique user counts but high engagement

### Limited Free Tiers

**4. Heap**: 10K sessions/month (very limiting)
**5. Pendo**: 500 MAUs (almost unusable for real products)
**6. LogRocket**: 1K sessions/month (proof of concept only)
**7. FullStory**: No free tier (14-day trial only)
**8. June**: No free tier

## Pricing Model Comparison

### Event-Based Pricing
**Providers**: PostHog, Mixpanel

**Pros**:
- Predictable for high-engagement apps (many events per user)
- No penalty for analyzing returning users repeatedly
- Transparent per-unit costs

**Cons**:
- Can be expensive for apps with very high event frequency
- Must carefully manage event volume

**Best for**: Apps with moderate event frequency, clear event taxonomy

---

### MTU-Based Pricing (Monthly Tracked Users)
**Providers**: Amplitude, LogRocket

**Pros**:
- Predictable for apps with defined user base
- Unlimited events per user (no event sampling)
- Good for high-engagement apps

**Cons**:
- Less predictable for traffic spikes
- Every unique user counts, even single visit
- Can be expensive for high-traffic B2C apps

**Best for**: B2B SaaS with known user counts, apps with deep per-user engagement

---

### Session-Based Pricing
**Providers**: Heap, FullStory

**Pros**:
- Natural unit for web analytics
- Predictable for known traffic patterns

**Cons**:
- Session definitions vary (30-min timeout typical)
- Can be expensive for high-frequency usage apps
- Less predictable than event-based

**Best for**: Content sites, e-commerce with session-based interactions

---

### MAU-Based Pricing (Monthly Active Users)
**Providers**: Pendo

**Pros**:
- Simple to understand
- Aligns with product growth metrics

**Cons**:
- Expensive for B2C with millions of users
- Must carefully define "active"

**Best for**: B2B SaaS with moderate user bases

## Hidden Costs & Considerations

### Add-Ons to Watch
- **Session Replay**: Often separate cost (PostHog includes, Mixpanel add-on, FullStory core)
- **Data Pipelines**: Warehouse export can cost extra
- **Group Analytics**: B2B features may require add-on (Mixpanel)
- **Advanced Analytics**: Funnels, custom dashboards may be add-ons (FullStory)

### Volume Overages
- **PostHog**: Set billing limits (never surprised)
- **Mixpanel**: Pay-per-use above plan limits
- **Amplitude**: Typically need to upgrade tier
- **Others**: Varies, check contract terms

### Annual Commitments
- Typical discounts: 20-30% off for annual vs monthly
- PostHog: No long-term commitment required
- Most others: Encourage annual contracts

## ROI Considerations

### Cost Per Insight
Consider total cost of ownership:
- Tool subscription cost
- Implementation time (engineering hours)
- Ongoing maintenance (event taxonomy, instrumentation updates)
- Training and onboarding
- Analyst time to generate insights

**PostHog Advantage**: Lower implementation burden (autocapture, all-in-one) reduces total cost

**Amplitude/Mixpanel Advantage**: More mature features may reduce analyst time for complex analyses

### Tool Consolidation Savings
**PostHog**: Replaces analytics + session replay + feature flags + A/B testing (4 tools in one)
**Pendo**: Replaces analytics + user guides + NPS + roadmap (4 tools in one)
**Single-purpose tools**: May need multiple subscriptions
