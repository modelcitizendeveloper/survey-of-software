# S3 Need-Driven: Use Case Matching

**Date**: November 2, 2025
**Purpose**: Match company profiles to ERP platforms with specific use case scenarios
**Format**: "If you are [profile], choose [ERP] because [reasoning]"

---

## I. By Industry

### Manufacturing (Discrete)

#### Light Manufacturing / Assembly
**Profile**: 50-200 employees, $10M-$100M revenue, assembly operations, basic BOMs

**Recommended ERP**: **Dynamics 365 BC Premium** ($110/user/mo)

**Why**:
- Strong manufacturing module (work orders, BOMs, routing)
- Best value for mid-market manufacturers
- AI features (Copilot) for production planning
- Power BI for manufacturing analytics
- **5-year TCO**: $575K (75 users)

**Alternative**: Acumatica ($460K TCO, unlimited users if large shop floor team)

---

#### Heavy Manufacturing / Complex MRP
**Profile**: 100-500 employees, $50M-$500M revenue, complex BOMs, multiple work centers, quality control critical

**Recommended ERP**: **SAP Business One** (Cloud: €91/user/mo)

**Why**:
- **Best manufacturing** features in SMB segment
- Advanced MRP, capacity planning, shop floor management
- Quality management (ISO 9001 support)
- Strong for pharma/medical devices (FDA 21 CFR Part 11)
- Handles manufacturing complexity better than Dynamics/NetSuite
- **5-year TCO**: $510K (75 users)

**Alternative**: Oracle ERP Cloud (if >$500M revenue, enterprise scale)

---

#### Engineer-to-Order / Job Shop
**Profile**: 25-150 employees, custom manufacturing, project-based, BOMs change per order

**Recommended ERP**: **Acumatica Manufacturing Edition** ($70K-$180K/year)

**Why**:
- Excellent project accounting + manufacturing
- Change order management
- Engineering change orders (ECO)
- Unlimited users (engineers, shop floor, project managers)
- **5-year TCO**: $460K (unlimited users)

**Alternative**: Dynamics 365 BC (better value if <50 users)

---

### Distribution / Wholesale

#### High-Volume Distribution
**Profile**: 75-300 employees, $25M-$250M revenue, large warehouse staff (50+ workers), high transaction volume

**Recommended ERP**: **Acumatica Distribution Edition** ($70K-$180K/year)

**Why**:
- **Consumption-based pricing = massive savings** vs NetSuite
- Unlimited users (critical for warehouse staff)
- Excellent WMS (wave picking, mobile scanning, zones)
- Strong order management and fulfillment
- **5-year TCO**: $460K vs $880K (NetSuite) = **$420K savings**
- **ROI**: 48% cheaper with better WMS

**Why Not NetSuite**: Per-user model costs $130K/year for 75 users vs $70K Acumatica

---

#### E-Commerce + Distribution
**Profile**: 30-150 employees, $10M-$100M revenue, online store + warehouse, omnichannel

**Recommended ERP**: **NetSuite** ($150K-$250K first year) or **Odoo** ($15K-$70K/year)

**NetSuite** if:
- Budget >$150K/year
- Planning to scale to $100M+ revenue
- Need best-in-class e-commerce (SuiteCommerce)
- Multi-country operations

**Odoo** if:
- Budget <$100K/year
- Have technical team (Python developers)
- Want native e-commerce (Odoo eCommerce)
- Flexible/customizable platform

**Why Not Dynamics BC**: No native e-commerce (requires Shopify integration)

---

### Professional Services

#### Consulting / Engineering Firms
**Profile**: 25-200 employees, $5M-$100M revenue, project-based billing, time & materials

**Recommended ERP**: **Acumatica Professional Services Edition** ($70K-$180K/year)

**Why**:
- **Best project accounting** in mid-market
- Time tracking, expense management
- Flexible billing (T&M, fixed fee, milestones, retainers)
- Resource management and utilization tracking
- Unlimited users (project managers, consultants, admin)
- **5-year TCO**: $350K-$460K

**Alternative**: Dynamics 365 BC (good project features at lower cost if <50 users)

---

### Construction

#### General Contractors / Specialty Trades
**Profile**: 50-300 employees, $10M-$200M revenue, job costing, subcontractors, AIA billing

**Recommended ERP**: **Acumatica Construction Edition** ($95K-$180K/year)

**Why**:
- **Purpose-built for construction**
- Job costing, change orders, subcontractor management
- AIA billing, compliance, certified payroll
- Unlimited users (field workers, subs, PMs)
- Mobile app for field data collection
- **5-year TCO**: $460K-$680K

**Alternative**: Dynamics 365 BC (adequate, cheaper for small contractors <50 employees)

**Why Not NetSuite**: Lacks construction-specific features, expensive

---

### Retail / E-Commerce

#### Omnichannel Retail
**Profile**: 25-150 employees, $10M-$100M revenue, online + physical stores, inventory sync critical

**Recommended ERP**: **NetSuite** or **Odoo**

**NetSuite** if:
- Budget >$150K/year
- Need SuiteCommerce (native e-commerce)
- Multi-location retail operations
- Real-time inventory sync (web store + POS)

**Odoo** if:
- Budget <$100K/year
- Need Odoo eCommerce + Odoo POS
- Want flexibility and customization
- Have technical resources

**Why Not SAP B1**: Limited e-commerce support

---

### SaaS / Technology Companies

#### SaaS Companies (Planning IPO)
**Profile**: 50-500 employees, $10M-$500M revenue, subscription billing, multi-entity, SOX compliance critical

**Recommended ERP**: **NetSuite** ($150K-$500K/year)

**Why**:
- **Industry standard for SaaS IPOs**
- Best ASC 606 revenue recognition
- SaaS metrics and dashboards (MRR, ARR, churn, LTV)
- Multi-entity consolidation (parent + subsidiaries)
- SOX compliance (audit trails, controls)
- Proven for public company readiness
- **Most SaaS unicorns use NetSuite**

**Alternative**: Dynamics 365 BC (if not planning IPO soon, budget <$100K/year)

**Why Not Odoo**: Lacks SOX sophistication, not proven for public companies

---

#### Early-Stage SaaS (<$10M revenue)
**Profile**: 10-50 employees, $1M-$10M revenue, subscription billing, fast growth

**Recommended ERP**: **Dynamics 365 BC** ($50K-$100K/year) or **Odoo** ($15K-$70K/year)

**Dynamics 365 BC** if:
- Want proven platform that scales to IPO
- Microsoft ecosystem (Azure, 365, Teams)
- AI features (Copilot) for financial insights
- Subscription billing via add-on

**Odoo** if:
- Very tight budget (<$50K/year)
- Have technical team
- Want maximum flexibility
- Open source important

**Why Not NetSuite**: Too expensive for early-stage ($150K+ first year)

## II. By Company Size

### Micro Business (1-10 employees, <$1M revenue)
**Recommendation**: **Not ready for ERP** - Use accounting software

**Use Instead**:
- QuickBooks Online ($30-$200/mo)
- Xero ($13-$70/mo)
- FreshBooks ($19-$60/mo)

**When to consider ERP**: Revenue >$5M or manufacturing/inventory needs

[See 3.006 Accounting Software research]

---

### Small Business (10-50 employees, $1M-$25M revenue)

**Best Choice**: **Dynamics 365 BC Essentials** ($80/user/mo)

**Why**:
- Most affordable full ERP ($24K-$48K/year for 10-30 users)
- Excellent features (financials, inventory, manufacturing)
- Copilot AI included free
- Scales to mid-market as you grow
- **5-year TCO**: $104K-$205K

**Budget Alternative**: **Odoo Enterprise** ($25/user/mo = $7.5K-$30K/year)
- 3x cheaper than Dynamics
- Requires technical resources or good partner
- More flexible but less polished

---

### Mid-Market (50-500 employees, $25M-$500M revenue)

**Best Value**: **Dynamics 365 BC Premium** ($110/user/mo)
- $155K-$575K 5-year TCO (depending on users)
- Best price-to-feature ratio
- Scales to $500M+ revenue

**Best for Manufacturing**: **SAP Business One** (€91/user/mo)
- $510K 5-year TCO (75 users)
- Superior manufacturing features

**Best for Distribution (Unlimited Users)**: **Acumatica** ($70K-$180K/year)
- $460K 5-year TCO
- Saves $420K vs NetSuite (75 users)

**Industry Standard (SaaS/Global)**: **NetSuite** ($150K-$500K/year)
- $880K-$3.15M 5-year TCO
- Premium pricing, proven at scale

---

### Large Enterprise (500+ employees, $500M+ revenue)

**Recommendation**: **Oracle ERP Cloud** ($2M-$10M+/year) or **NetSuite** (large deployment)

**Oracle ERP Cloud** if:
- >$500M revenue
- Complex consolidation (1,000+ entities)
- Regulated industry (healthcare, pharma, energy)
- Need FedRAMP (federal government)
- Budget $5M-$10M+ first year

**NetSuite** if:
- $100M-$500M revenue (mid-market/enterprise bridge)
- SaaS/e-commerce company
- Don't need enterprise complexity
- Budget $500K-$2M/year

## IV. By Special Requirements

### Requirement: Unlimited Users Needed

**Scenario**: 150-person company, 100 warehouse/field workers need access

**Recommendation**: **Acumatica** ($70K-$180K/year)

**Cost Comparison**:
| Platform | Cost (100 users) | Notes |
|----------|------------------|-------|
| Acumatica | $70K-$180K/year | Unlimited users (consumption pricing) |
| NetSuite | $155K-$200K/year | 100 users × $129/mo = $154K + base $30K |
| Dynamics BC | $132K/year | 100 users × $110/mo |
| SAP B1 | $120K/year | 100 users × $100/mo (cloud) |

**Savings**: $50K-$80K/year with Acumatica = **$250K-$400K over 5 years**

---

### Requirement: Must Be On-Premise (Data Sovereignty)

**Options**:
1. **SAP Business One** (on-premise mature)
2. **Odoo Community** (self-hosted)
3. **Acumatica** (private cloud or on-premise)
4. **Dynamics 365 BC** (on-premise available but not recommended)

**Best**: **SAP Business One** (most mature on-premise ERP)

**Why Not**: NetSuite, Oracle ERP Cloud (cloud-only)

---

### Requirement: Open Source / No Vendor Lock-In

**Recommendation**: **Odoo Community Edition** (self-hosted)

**Why**:
- Open source (LGPL license)
- Full source code access
- Data portability (PostgreSQL database)
- Can fork if needed
- Community ecosystem (50,000+ modules)

**Trade-offs**:
- No official support (community forums only)
- Self-hosted infrastructure costs
- Implementation partner quality varies

---

### Requirement: Native E-Commerce Platform

**Options**:
1. **NetSuite** (SuiteCommerce) - Best, expensive
2. **Odoo** (Odoo eCommerce) - Best value
3. **Acumatica** (Commerce Edition) - Good, via connector

**Not Suitable**: Dynamics BC, SAP B1 (require Shopify/WooCommerce integration)

---

### Requirement: SOX Compliance (Going Public)

**Recommendation**: **NetSuite** (industry standard for SaaS IPOs)

**Why**:
- Best audit trails and change management
- SOX controls out-of-box
- Proven for public company transitions (hundreds of IPOs)
- Multi-entity consolidation for parent + subs
- SaaS companies trust NetSuite for IPO readiness

**Alternative**: Dynamics 365 BC (adequate SOX, cheaper, but less proven for IPOs)

**Why Not**: Odoo (lacks SOX sophistication)

---

### Requirement: Best AI Features

**Recommendation**: **Dynamics 365 BC** (Copilot included free)

**Why**:
- Generative AI for financial analysis
- Account reconciliation AI
- Natural language reporting
- E-invoicing automation
- Predictive cash flow
- **Included at no extra cost**

**Alternative**: Oracle ERP Cloud (comprehensive AI, but $2M+/year)

**Why Not**: NetSuite (surprisingly weak AI), SAP B1 (minimal AI), Acumatica (limited AI)

---

### Requirement: Microsoft Ecosystem Integration

**Recommendation**: **Dynamics 365 BC** (native Microsoft platform)

**Why**:
- Native Microsoft 365 integration (Outlook, Excel, Teams, SharePoint)
- Power Platform (Power Apps, Power Automate, Power BI)
- Azure integration
- Same authentication (Azure AD/Entra)
- Unified support (Microsoft Premier)

**Why Not**: Other platforms require integration vs native

---

### Requirement: Best for Global Operations (100+ countries)

**Recommendation**: **NetSuite** (190+ currencies, 100+ countries, 27+ languages)

**Why**:
- Best multi-entity consolidation
- Strong localization (tax, statutory reporting)
- Multi-currency real-time
- Global subsidiaries management
- Proven for international operations

**Alternative**: Oracle ERP Cloud (enterprise scale, 29 regions)

## V. Migration Paths

### From QuickBooks → ERP

**Small Manufacturing**: QuickBooks Desktop → **Dynamics 365 BC** ($50K-$100K first year)

**Growing SaaS**: QuickBooks Online → **Dynamics 365 BC** or **NetSuite** (if $10M+ revenue)

**Distribution**: QuickBooks Enterprise → **Acumatica** (unlimited users) or **Dynamics 365 BC**

**Budget-Conscious**: QuickBooks → **Odoo** ($15K-$70K/year)

---

### From Xero → ERP

**Profile**: Typically fast-growing tech/services companies

**Best Path**: Xero → **NetSuite** (if $10M+ revenue, VC-backed) or **Dynamics 365 BC** (if <$50M revenue)

**Why**: Both scale well beyond Xero's limitations

---

### From Sage 100/300 → Modern ERP

**Manufacturing**: Sage → **SAP Business One** (similar complexity, modern platform)

**Services**: Sage → **Dynamics 365 BC** (easier migration, better value)

**Distribution**: Sage → **Acumatica** (unlimited users, modern UX)

---

### From Legacy Microsoft Dynamics GP/NAV → Dynamics 365 BC

**Profile**: Existing Microsoft Dynamics customers

**Best Path**: GP/NAV → **Dynamics 365 BC**

**Why**:
- Microsoft upgrade path (tools and partners available)
- Familiar interface and workflows
- Data migration easier (same vendor)
- Keep Microsoft ecosystem integration

**Alternative**: Consider **NetSuite** or **Acumatica** for fresh start (if dissatisfied with Microsoft)

---

### From Oracle E-Business Suite → Cloud ERP

**Enterprise**: E-Business Suite → **Oracle ERP Cloud** (Oracle's migration path)

**Mid-Market**: E-Business Suite → **NetSuite** (simpler, faster, cheaper)

**Why**: Oracle customers often downsize from E-Business Suite (overkill for most)

## VI. Decision Trees

### Decision Tree #1: Manufacturing Company

```
START: Are you a manufacturer?
│
├─ Q1: How complex is your manufacturing?
│   ├─ Light assembly, basic BOMs → Dynamics 365 BC
│   ├─ Complex MRP, quality control → SAP Business One
│   └─ Engineer-to-order, job shop → Acumatica
│
└─ Q2: What's your budget (first year)?
    ├─ <$100K → Odoo Enterprise
    ├─ $100K-$200K → Dynamics 365 BC or SAP Business One
    └─ >$200K → NetSuite or Acumatica
```

---

### Decision Tree #2: Distribution Company

```
START: Are you in distribution/wholesale?
│
├─ Q1: How many warehouse workers?
│   ├─ <25 workers → Dynamics 365 BC (per-user OK)
│   ├─ 25-100 workers → Acumatica (unlimited users)
│   └─ >100 workers → Acumatica (consumption pricing saves $$$)
│
└─ Q2: Do you need e-commerce?
    ├─ Yes, native → NetSuite (SuiteCommerce) or Odoo (Odoo eCommerce)
    └─ No, or integrate Shopify → Dynamics 365 BC or Acumatica
```

---

### Decision Tree #3: Professional Services

```
START: Are you a services firm?
│
├─ Q1: Do you need project accounting?
│   ├─ Critical (T&M billing, project P&L) → Acumatica Professional Services
│   └─ Basic (simple time tracking) → Dynamics 365 BC
│
└─ Q2: How many project staff?
    ├─ <50 → Dynamics 365 BC (cheaper per-user)
    └─ >50 → Acumatica (unlimited users)
```

---

### Decision Tree #4: SaaS Company

```
START: Are you a SaaS/subscription company?
│
├─ Q1: Are you planning to go public (IPO)?
│   ├─ Yes, within 2-3 years → NetSuite (SOX, proven for IPOs)
│   └─ No plans → Dynamics 365 BC (better value)
│
├─ Q2: What's your revenue?
│   ├─ <$10M → Dynamics 365 BC or Odoo
│   ├─ $10M-$100M → NetSuite or Dynamics 365 BC
│   └─ >$100M → NetSuite
│
└─ Q3: Multi-entity complexity?
    ├─ Parent + 5+ subsidiaries → NetSuite (best consolidation)
    └─ Single entity or simple structure → Dynamics 365 BC
```

---

## VII. Key Takeaways

1. **Dynamics 365 BC** = Best value for 80% of mid-market companies ($25M-$100M revenue)
2. **Acumatica** = Best for unlimited users (distribution, construction, field service)
3. **SAP Business One** = Best for heavy manufacturing (complex MRP, quality control)
4. **NetSuite** = Best for SaaS IPOs and global operations
5. **Odoo** = Best for budget-conscious companies with technical resources
6. **Oracle ERP Cloud** = Only for enterprises >$500M revenue with complex needs

**Most Common Choice**: **Dynamics 365 BC** (best features-to-price ratio + AI)
