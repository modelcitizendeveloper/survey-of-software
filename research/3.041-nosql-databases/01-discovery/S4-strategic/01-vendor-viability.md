## Vendor Viability Matrix (5-Year Survival)

### Tier 1: Extremely Stable (99%+ 5-year survival)

**Cloud Provider Managed Services:**
- **DynamoDB (AWS):** 99.9% - Amazon core infrastructure
- **Firestore (Google Cloud):** 99% - Google core product
- **Cosmos DB (Azure):** 99% - Microsoft core product

**Rationale:** Cloud providers won't shut down core database services (too critical to ecosystem)

**Risk:** Product evolution/pricing changes, not shutdown

---

- Customers: 47,000+ (including Fortune 500)
- Valuation: $25B+ market cap

**Redis Ltd.**

**DataStax**
- **Likely acquirers:** Oracle, IBM, Cisco
- **Probability:** 40%

## Red Flags to Watch

### None of the analyzed providers show red flags, but monitor:

**For VC-backed companies:**
- ❌ Revenue decline (none showing this)
- ❌ Customer churn >20% (none showing this)
- ❌ Failed funding rounds (none showing this)
- ❌ Major customer losses (none showing this)

**For cloud providers:**
- ⚠️ Product deprecation announcements (rare but possible)
- ⚠️ Major price increases (happens periodically)
- ⚠️ Feature stagnation (competitive pressure prevents this)

**For acquired companies:**
- ⚠️ Post-acquisition product neglect (historical examples: Sun MySQL → Oracle)
- ⚠️ Forced migration to acquirer's platform (rare in database space)
