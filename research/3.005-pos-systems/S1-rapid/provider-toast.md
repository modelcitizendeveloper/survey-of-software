## Key Strengths

### 1. Restaurant-Specific Design
- **Built for food service:** Every feature designed for restaurants
- **Table management:** Visual floor plans, table status, server sections
- **Menu modifiers:** Complex customization (no onions, extra cheese, etc.)
- **Kitchen routing:** Send orders to specific prep stations
- **Recipe costing:** Track ingredient-level costs and margins

### 2. Comprehensive Restaurant Features
- **Order management:** Dine-in, takeout, delivery unified
- **Split bills:** By item, by seat, by amount
- **Tip management:** Pooling, distribution, reporting
- **Course firing:** Send appetizers, hold entrees, manage timing
- **86'ing items:** Mark items out of stock during service

### 3. Kitchen Display System (KDS)
- Replace paper tickets with digital screens
- Color-coded order priorities
- Bump bars for order completion
- Expediter view for managing multiple stations
- Reduces errors and speeds up service

### 4. Online Ordering Integration
- Commission-free online ordering (Toast-branded)
- QR code ordering for dine-in
- Integration with DoorDash, Uber Eats, Grubhub
- Unified menu management across all channels

### 5. Robust Reporting
- Real-time sales dashboards
- Labor cost analysis
- Menu item performance
- Server performance tracking
- P&L reports with food cost tracking

### 6. Spill-Resistant Hardware
- Designed for restaurant environments
- Withstands heat, moisture, and splatter
- More durable than consumer-grade tablets

## Key Weaknesses

### 1. Proprietary Hardware Lock-In
- Must use Toast-branded hardware
- Cannot use standard iPads or Android tablets
- Expensive to replace if damaged
- Switching POS means replacing all hardware

### 2. Payment Processing Lock-In
- Required to use Toast Payments
- Cannot bring your own processor
- No ability to shop around for better rates

### 3. Higher Costs Than General-Purpose POS
- Hardware more expensive than Square or Clover
- Software starts at $69/mo (no truly free plan)
- Many features require paid add-ons
- Total cost can add up quickly

### 4. Contract Commitments
- Typically 2-year contracts
- Early termination fees (can be significant)
- Less flexibility than month-to-month competitors

### 5. Add-On Pricing Complexity
- Core features included in base plan
- Many advanced features cost extra:
  - Payroll ($90/mo + per employee)
  - Gift cards (transaction fees)
  - Loyalty program (additional cost)
  - Advanced inventory (add-on)
  - Catering module (add-on)
- Final monthly cost can exceed initial quote

### 6. Not Suitable for Non-Restaurant Businesses
- Retail inventory management very limited
- No appointment booking
- Product variants not well-supported
- Designed exclusively for food service

- ✅ **Full-service restaurants** with table management needs

## Decision Framework

**Choose Toast if:**
- You operate a full-service restaurant with table management
- You need a comprehensive kitchen display system
- You process high volume (lower rates offset software cost)
- You want restaurant-specific features over general-purpose POS
- You're comfortable with 2-year contract commitment
- You value integrated online ordering and delivery

**Choose a competitor if:**
- You're a small cafe or food truck on tight budget (Square)

- You run a retail or service business (not a restaurant)
- You're unwilling to commit to contracts (Square)
- You want lower upfront hardware costs (Square)

## Cost Comparison Example

**Hypothetical Scenario:** Full-service restaurant, 2 terminals, 10 employees, $50,000/month revenue

### Toast Costs (Monthly)
- Software: $69/mo
- Payroll bundle: $90 + ($9 × 10) = $180/mo
- Processing (2.49% on $50K): $1,245/mo
- **Total: ~$1,494/mo** + hardware ($1,500-$3,000 upfront)

### Square Costs (Monthly)
- Software: $69/mo (Restaurant Plus)
- Payroll: Via Square Payroll (~$150/mo estimate)
- Processing (2.6% on $50K): $1,300/mo
- **Total: ~$1,519/mo** + hardware ($600-$1,000 upfront)

**Analysis:** Similar monthly cost, but Toast offers more restaurant-specific features while Square offers more flexibility.
