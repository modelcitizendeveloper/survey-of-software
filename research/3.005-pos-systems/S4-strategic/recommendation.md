## Executive Summary

For long-term POS commitments (3-5 year planning horizon), prioritize **vendor stability** and **low lock-in risk** over feature richness.

**Top Strategic Choices (by business type):**

1. **Square** - Lowest lock-in risk, highest vendor stability (public company Block Inc., NYSE: SQ)

5. **Clover** - Backed by Fiserv (Fortune 500), stable but medium lock-in

## Strategic Recommendation by Scenario

### **Scenario 1: Startup/New Business (unsure of future)**
**Recommendation:** **Square Free or Square Plus**
- **Why:** Zero lock-in, month-to-month, can switch if business model changes
- **Risk:** If you grow into a restaurant needing table management, will outgrow Square
- **Mitigation:** Test with Square for 6-12 months, migrate to vertical-specific if needed

### **Scenario 2: Restaurant (committed to industry)**
**Recommendation:** **Toast** (if full-service) or **Square** (if QSR/casual)
- **Why:** Toast lock-in justified by best restaurant features
- **Risk:** Locked into Toast ecosystem, expensive to leave
- **Mitigation:** Negotiate contract terms carefully (avoid 3-year if uncertain)

### **Scenario 3: Retail + E-commerce (omnichannel)**
**Recommendation:** **Shopify POS**
- **Why:** Unified inventory, best omnichannel experience
- **Risk:** Shopify Payments lock-in (2.7% rate non-negotiable for small business)
- **Mitigation:** TCO higher but justified by e-commerce synergy

### **Scenario 4: Multi-Location Retail (5+ stores)**
**Recommendation:** **Lightspeed Retail**
- **Why:** Best multi-location inventory, reporting, centralized management
- **Risk:** Higher cost, steeper learning curve
- **Mitigation:** ROI justified at scale (5+ locations, $5M+ revenue)

### **Scenario 5: Risk-Averse (enterprise/government)**
**Recommendation:** **Clover** (Fiserv-backed) or **Square** (public company)
- **Why:** Parent company stability, compliance certifications
- **Risk:** Clover has medium lock-in
- **Mitigation:** Negotiate favorable contract terms upfront

## Red Flags (Avoid These Strategically)

‚ùå **3-year contracts without exit clause** (Toast, some Clover contracts)
‚ùå **Proprietary hardware you can't resell** (Toast terminals, NCR Aloha)
‚ùå **Locked payment processing with no alternatives** (Toast Payments required)
‚ùå **Limited data export capabilities** (some legacy systems)
‚ùå **VC-backed startups with <$50M revenue** (acquisition/shutdown risk)
‚ùå **Processing fees >3% for card-present** (negotiation failure)
‚ùå **Monthly fees >$200 for single location** (unless enterprise features needed)

### **Low-Risk Migration** (Square ‚Üí Shopify, Square ‚Üí Clover)
- **Timeline:** 2-4 weeks
- **Cost:** $500-$2,000 (setup, training)
- **Complexity:** LOW (export data, import to new system, parallel test)

### **Medium-Risk Migration** (Toast ‚Üí Square, Lightspeed ‚Üí Shopify)
- **Timeline:** 1-3 months
- **Cost:** $3,000-$8,000 (hardware replacement, data migration, training, downtime)
- **Complexity:** MEDIUM (vertical-specific features may need workarounds)

### **High-Risk Migration** (Toast ‚Üí Lightspeed, Clover ‚Üí Toast)
- **Timeline:** 3-6 months

- **Complexity:** HIGH (ecosystem switch, payment processor change, integration rewiring)

## Final Strategic Guidance

### **Choose Low Lock-In IF:**
- Early-stage business (< 2 years old)
- Uncertain about future business model
- Value flexibility over features
- Budget-conscious, bootstrapped
- **‚Üí Square, Shopify POS, TouchBistro**

### **Accept Medium Lock-In IF:**
- Established business (2+ years)
- Clear on vertical (restaurant or retail)
- Value best-in-class features
- Can commit 12-24 months
- **‚Üí Toast (restaurants), Lightspeed (retail), Clover (customization)**

### **Only Accept High Lock-In IF:**
- Enterprise/multi-location (5+ stores)
- Custom requirements (extensive API, integrations)
- Willing to commit 3+ years
- Have migration budget ($10K+) if needed
- **‚Üí Revel, NCR, custom enterprise systems**

---

## Conclusion

**For 90% of businesses:** Start with **Square** (general) or **Shopify POS** (retail+online). Low lock-in allows you to learn what you actually need before committing to specialized platforms.

**For restaurants committed to industry:** **Toast** lock-in is justified IF you're certain about full-service restaurant model for 3+ years.

**For complex retail/multi-location:** **Lightspeed** or **Shopify** provide advanced features worth the higher TCO.

**Avoid:** Long-term contracts, proprietary hardware, and locked payment processing unless absolutely certain about 3-5 year commitment.

---

**Strategic Selection Principle:** Optimize for **optionality** in early stages, commit to **specialization** once business model is validated and stable.
