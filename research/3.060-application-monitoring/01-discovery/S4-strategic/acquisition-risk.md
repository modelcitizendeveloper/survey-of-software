## Customer Impact Analysis: Post-Acquisition

**Typical Acquisition Timeline**:
- **0-6 months**: Announcement, customer communication, contract continuity
- **6-12 months**: Product integration planning, pricing changes announced
- **12-24 months**: Feature migration begins, forced customer migrations
- **24-36 months**: Standalone product sunset, full integration complete

**Common Post-Acquisition Changes**:
1. **Pricing Increases**: 30-50% within first 12 months
2. **Feature Deprecation**: Standalone features moved to acquirer platform
3. **Contract Changes**: New terms, enterprise minimums, bundling requirements
4. **Support Degradation**: Reduced support quality during transition
5. **Migration Mandates**: Forced migration to acquirer platform within 24 months

**Customer Protection Strategies**:
- Negotiate acquisition protection clauses (pricing continuity, early termination rights)
- Maintain migration runbooks for alternative providers
- Implement provider abstraction layers (easy switching)
- Avoid multi-year contracts with high-risk providers (Rollbar)
- Regular data exports (error history, configurations)
