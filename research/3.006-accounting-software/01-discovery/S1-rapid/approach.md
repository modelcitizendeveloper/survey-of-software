# S1: Rapid Search - Approach

**Goal:** Identify the top 10-15 accounting software platforms across small business to mid-market segments

**Time Budget:** 30-45 minutes

**Methodology:**
1. Segment by market size (small business vs mid-market)
2. Identify market leaders in each segment
3. Quick capture of key differentiators
4. Document basic pricing tiers
5. Note target customer size/profile

---

## Market Segmentation

### Small Business Accounting (Freelancers to 50 employees, <$5M revenue)
**Focus:** Ease of use, affordability, quick setup, accountant ecosystem

**Top Platforms to Research:**
- QuickBooks Online (Intuit) - Market leader
- Xero - Strong international presence
- FreshBooks - Service businesses
- Wave - Free option
- Zoho Books - Budget option
- QuickBooks Desktop - Legacy option
- Sage Business Cloud Accounting (Sage 50cloud)

### Mid-Market Accounting (50-500 employees, $5M-$100M revenue)
**Focus:** Multi-entity consolidation, advanced reporting, scalability, role-based access

**Top Platforms to Research:**
- Sage Intacct - Cloud-native mid-market leader
- NetSuite (Oracle) - ERP with strong accounting
- Microsoft Dynamics 365 Business Central - Microsoft ecosystem
- Acumatica - Cloud ERP with accounting focus

### Niche/Specialty Platforms (Brief mention only)
- Kashoo - Small business, simple
- GnuCash - Open source, desktop
- AccountEdge - Mac-focused
- Bench - Bookkeeping service + software

---

## Key Information to Capture

For each platform:
1. **Target Market:** Business size, industry focus
2. **Pricing:** Starting price, typical monthly cost
3. **Deployment:** Cloud, desktop, or hybrid
4. **Key Strengths:** What makes it stand out
5. **Key Weaknesses:** What it lacks or who it doesn't fit
6. **Typical Users:** Who chooses this platform

---

## Success Criteria

S1 complete when:
- ✅ 10-15 platforms documented
- ✅ Clear segmentation by market size
- ✅ Pricing ranges captured
- ✅ Quick recommendations possible (which platform for which user)
- ✅ Ready for S2 deep dive
