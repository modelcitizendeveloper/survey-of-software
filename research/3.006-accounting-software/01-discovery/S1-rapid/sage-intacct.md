# Sage Intacct

**Provider:** Sage (FTSE 100 listed company)
**Category:** Mid-Market Accounting
**Target Market:** Growing businesses ($4M-$100M revenue, 20-500 employees)

## Overview

Sage Intacct is the leading cloud-native mid-market accounting platform, offering advanced features for businesses outgrowing QuickBooks/Xero. Purpose-built for multi-entity, multi-currency operations with sophisticated reporting.

## Pricing (2025)

**Custom Quote-Based Pricing**
- No published pricing tiers
- Pricing factors:
  - Number and type of users (full vs. limited access)
  - Modules selected (core financials vs. advanced modules)
  - Number of entities managed
  - Depth of customization needed
  - Scale of implementation

**Typical Costs:**
- **Base annual subscription:** Starting ~$15,000/year
- **Implementation costs:** 1.0-1.5x first-year subscription ($15,000-$22,500)
- **Total Year 1:** $30,000-$50,000+ for mid-sized deployment

**Modules (add-on costs):**
- Core Financials (standard)
- Multi-Entity Management
- Advanced Project Accounting
- Inventory Management
- Order Management
- Subscription Billing
- Revenue Recognition (ASC 606)

## Key Strengths

1. **Cloud-native** - Built for cloud from ground up (unlike NetSuite)
2. **Multi-entity consolidation** - Best-in-class multi-entity management
3. **Dimensional reporting** - Sophisticated financial dimensions and reporting
4. **Automation** - Strong AP automation and workflow capabilities
5. **Project accounting** - Excellent for professional services and project-based businesses
6. **Partner ecosystem** - 350+ integrations via marketplace
7. **Scalability** - Grows with business without needing to re-implement

## Key Weaknesses

1. **Price** - Significantly more expensive than small business solutions
2. **Implementation complexity** - Requires professional services, 2-6 month implementation
3. **Overkill for small business** - Too much for businesses <$4M revenue
4. **Learning curve** - More complex than QuickBooks/Xero
5. **Not a full ERP** - Accounting-focused, lacks manufacturing/supply chain depth

## Best Fit For

- **Growing companies** outgrowing QuickBooks/Xero ($4M-$100M revenue)
- **Multi-entity businesses** needing consolidation (2+ legal entities)
- **Professional services firms** needing advanced project accounting
- **SaaS companies** requiring ASC 606 revenue recognition
- **Nonprofits** (has strong fund accounting capabilities)
- **Businesses needing sophisticated reporting** but not full ERP

## Competitive Position

- **vs QuickBooks/Xero:** Mid-market vs small business; advanced features vs simplicity
- **vs NetSuite:** Accounting-focused vs full ERP; easier implementation
- **vs Dynamics 365:** Independent vs Microsoft ecosystem lock-in
- **vs Full ERP:** Accounting excellence without manufacturing/supply chain complexity

## Integration Highlights

- 350+ apps via Sage Intacct Marketplace

## Graduation Path

**When businesses choose Sage Intacct:**
- Outgrowing QuickBooks/Xero (multi-entity complexity)
- Revenue $4M-$100M range
- Need better financial reporting and consolidation
- Don't need full ERP (no manufacturing)
- Want cloud-native solution

**When to graduate FROM Sage Intacct:**
- Revenue exceeds $100M+ (NetSuite/SAP territory)
- Need full ERP (manufacturing, complex supply chain)
- Require industry-specific ERP features
- Need deeper operational capabilities

**Next step:** NetSuite, SAP, or Oracle ERP Cloud (full ERP)
