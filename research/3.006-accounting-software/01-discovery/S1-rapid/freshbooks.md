# FreshBooks

**Provider:** FreshBooks (Private, Toronto-based)
**Category:** Small Business Accounting
**Target Market:** Service-based businesses, freelancers, consultants

## Overview

FreshBooks is designed specifically for service-based businesses, offering exceptional invoicing, time tracking, and project management features. Best for businesses billing by the hour or project.

## Pricing (2025)

**Lite:** $19/month (or $17.10/month annual)
- Up to 5 billable clients
- Unlimited invoices, estimates, expenses
- Time tracking and mileage

**Plus:** $33/month (or $29.70/month annual)
- Up to 50 billable clients
- Unlimited proposals
- Project profitability reporting

**Premium:** $60/month (or $54/month annual)
- Unlimited billable clients
- 2 team members included
- Advanced reporting

**Select:** Custom pricing
- Customizable features for larger businesses
- Contact sales for quote

**Add-Ons:**
- Additional users: $11/month per user
- Advanced Payments: $20/month
- 30-day free trial available

## Key Strengths

1. **Service business focus** - Purpose-built for hourly/project billing
2. **Time tracking** - Best-in-class time tracking integrated into invoicing
3. **Ease of use** - Simplest learning curve in category
4. **Proposals** - Beautiful proposal creation and tracking
5. **Client portal** - Clients can view projects, invoices, and communicate
6. **Project management** - Strong project tracking for service work

## Key Weaknesses

1. **Client limits** - Lite and Plus tiers have billable client caps
2. **Inventory** - No inventory management (not built for product businesses)
3. **Limited reporting** - Reporting less comprehensive than QuickBooks/Xero
4. **Scalability** - Not ideal for businesses with employees and complex needs
5. **Ecosystem** - Smaller integration ecosystem vs competitors
6. **Accountant network** - Fewer accountants specialize in FreshBooks

## Best Fit For

- **Service-based businesses** (consulting, agencies, law, design, marketing)
- **Freelancers and contractors** billing by the hour
- **Professional services** needing proposal and project tracking
- **Small teams** focused on client billable work
- **Businesses not needing inventory management**

## Competitive Position

- **vs QuickBooks:** Simpler, better for services; lacks inventory and depth
- **vs Xero:** More service-focused, easier to learn; less comprehensive accounting
- **vs Wave:** Full-featured paid solution with superior time tracking
- **vs Harvest:** FreshBooks adds full accounting vs Harvest's time tracking focus

## Integration Highlights

- Stripe, PayPal (payment processing)
- G Suite, Office 365 (productivity)
- Gusto (payroll)
- Zapier (workflow automation)
- Limited compared to QuickBooks/Xero ecosystem

## Graduation Path

**When to outgrow FreshBooks:**
- Add inventory/product sales to service business
- Need more complex financial reporting
- Scale beyond 10-15 employees
- Require multi-entity management
- Accountant requires QuickBooks/Xero specifically

**Next step:** QuickBooks Online or Xero (if staying small business), Sage Intacct (if going mid-market)

## Notes

- Includes project management features competitors charge extra for
- Mileage tracking built-in (useful for client visits)
- Client limits on lower tiers can force upgrades
- Not ideal for product-based or inventory businesses
- Strong for creative agencies and professional services
- Canadian company, strong North American presence
