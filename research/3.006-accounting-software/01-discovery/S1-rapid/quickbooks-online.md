# QuickBooks Online

**Provider:** Intuit
**Category:** Small Business Accounting
**Target Market:** Freelancers to small businesses (<50 employees, <$5M revenue)

## Overview

QuickBooks Online is the market leader in small business accounting software in North America, offering cloud-based accounting with extensive features and the largest accountant/bookkeeper ecosystem.

## Pricing (2025)

**Solopreneur:** $20/month
- Side gigs, separating business and personal finances
- 1 user

**Simple Start:** $38/month (promotional pricing often available)
- Small businesses and freelancers
- Income/expense tracking, invoicing, tax benefits
- 1 user

**Essentials:** ~$55/month
- Small teams needing time tracking and bill management
- Up to 3 users

**Plus:** ~$85/month
- Inventory management, project tracking, advanced reporting
- Up to 5 users

**Advanced:** $275/month
- Business insights, analytics tools
- Up to 25 users

**Add-Ons:**
- Payroll: $50-130/month base + $6-11/employee/month
- 30-day free trial available
- Frequent promotional discounts (50% off common)

## Key Strengths

1. **Market dominance** - Largest accountant/bookkeeper ecosystem
2. **Feature completeness** - Most comprehensive feature set for small business
3. **Ecosystem** - Deep integrations with banks, payment processors, apps
4. **Support** - Best support in category (phone, chat, resources)
5. **Inventory management** - Strong inventory tracking on Plus tier
6. **Scalability** - Grows from solopreneur to 25 users

## Key Weaknesses

1. **Price creep** - Regular price increases, add-on costs add up
2. **Complexity** - More complex than alternatives (can be overwhelming)
3. **Upsell pressure** - Constant prompts to upgrade tiers or add services
4. **International** - Weaker outside North America
5. **Mobile app** - Less polished than Xero's mobile experience

## Best Fit For

- **US-based small businesses** that value ecosystem compatibility
- **Businesses with inventory** (Plus tier has best inventory features)
- **Companies working with bookkeepers/accountants** (most professionals know QuickBooks)
- **Growing businesses** needing to scale to 5-25 users
- **Businesses needing payroll integration** (Intuit Payroll tight integration)

## Competitive Position

- **vs Xero:** More features, larger ecosystem, but more expensive and complex
- **vs FreshBooks:** More comprehensive, better inventory, but harder to learn
- **vs Wave:** Full-featured paid solution vs free basic option
- **vs Sage Intacct:** Small business focus vs mid-market scalability

## Integration Highlights

- Intuit PaymentNetwork (Stripe, Square)
- All major banks (Plaid integration)
- Shopify, WooCommerce (e-commerce)
- TSheets time tracking (Intuit-owned)
- Hundreds of third-party apps via App Store

## Graduation Path

**When to outgrow QuickBooks Online:**
- Multi-entity consolidation becomes complex (>3 entities)
- Need for advanced financial reporting/dashboards
- Revenue exceeds $10M or 50+ employees
- Require deeper ERP functionality
- International operations become substantial

**Next step:** Sage Intacct or NetSuite

## Notes

- QuickBooks Desktop still exists for offline/legacy users but cloud version recommended
- Strong for US tax compliance (1099s, sales tax, etc.)
- Dominant small business accounting choice in North America
- Accountant ecosystem is key competitive moat
