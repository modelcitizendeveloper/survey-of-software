# Wave

**Provider:** Wave Financial (H&R Block subsidiary)
**Category:** Small Business Accounting
**Target Market:** Micro businesses, freelancers, sole proprietors (<$100K revenue)

## Overview

Wave offers free accounting software with revenue from payment processing and paid add-ons. Best for very small businesses with simple needs and limited budgets.

## Pricing (2025)

**Free (Starter):**
- Unlimited invoices and estimates
- Income and expense tracking
- Bank reconciliation
- Basic financial reports
- Receipt scanning
- **Limitations:** Manual transaction import, no automation

**Pro:** $16/month ($170/year)
- Everything in Free, plus:
- Automatic bank transaction import and categorization
- Auto-merge duplicate transactions
- Automated late payment reminders
- Recurring invoices automation

**Additional Costs:**
- Payment processing: 2.9% + $0.60 per transaction
- Payroll: $20-40/month base + $4-6/employee
- No free trial (already has free plan)

## Key Strengths

1. **Price** - Core accounting is completely free
2. **Simplicity** - Very easy to learn and use
3. **Receipt scanning** - Mobile app for expense receipts
4. **Invoicing** - Professional invoices included free
5. **No user limits** - Unlimited users on free plan
6. **Revenue under $100K** - Perfect for micro businesses

## Key Weaknesses

1. **Advanced features** - Lacks inventory, time tracking, project costing
2. **Automation** - Most automation locked behind Pro ($16/mo)
3. **Support** - Limited support for free users
4. **Reporting** - Basic reports only, limited customization
5. **Integrations** - Very limited third-party integrations
6. **Scalability** - Not suitable for growing businesses
7. **Payroll** - Payroll limited to US/Canada only
8. **Double-entry** - No double-entry accounting option

## Best Fit For

- **Micro businesses** (<$100K revenue annually)
- **Freelancers and sole proprietors** with simple needs
- **Side businesses and hobbyists**
- **Budget-conscious startups** getting started
- **Service businesses without inventory**
- **Businesses with <10 employees**

## Competitive Position

- **vs QuickBooks:** Free vs paid; basic features vs comprehensive
- **vs Xero:** Free vs paid; simpler but limited features
- **vs FreshBooks:** Free vs paid; lacks time tracking and project features
- **vs Spreadsheets:** Structured accounting vs manual tracking

## Integration Highlights

- Payment processing (Wave Payments built-in)
- Payroll (Wave Payroll add-on)
- Limited third-party integrations
- No app marketplace
- Basic API available

## Graduation Path

**When to outgrow Wave:**
- Revenue exceeds $100K annually
- Need inventory management
- Need advanced reporting or customization
- Require time tracking or project costing
- Need better integrations (bank sync, apps)
- Accountant requires QuickBooks/Xero
- Need audit trails and compliance features

**Next step:** QuickBooks Online, Xero, or FreshBooks

## Notes

- Owned by H&R Block (acquired for tax product integration)
- Makes money from payment processing and payroll, not software
- Great starting point, but limited growth path
- Pro plan ($16/mo) adds crucial automation features
- North America only (US and Canada)
- Best for businesses that may stay micro permanently
- Reconciliation can be frustrating (user reviews note this frequently)
- No phone support for free users
