# Xero

**Provider:** Xero Limited (NZX/ASX listed)
**Category:** Small Business Accounting
**Target Market:** Small to growing businesses (freelancers to 100 employees)

## Overview

Xero is the cloud-native accounting platform with the strongest international presence, offering modern UX, unlimited users, and extensive app integrations. Market leader outside North America.

## Pricing (2025)

**Early (Starter):** $20/month (promotional: $2/mo for 3 months)
- Up to 20 invoices/quotes per month
- Reconcile up to 20 bank transactions per month
- Unlimited users

**Growing (Standard):** $42/month (promotional: $8/mo for 3 months)
- Unlimited invoices and quotes
- Unlimited bank transactions
- Bulk reconciliation
- Unlimited users

**Established (Premium):** $80/month
- Everything in Growing, plus:
- Multi-currency
- Expense claims
- Project tracking
- Unlimited users

**Promotions:**
- 50-90% off first 3-6 months common for new customers
- 14-day free trial available

## Key Strengths

1. **Unlimited users** - All plans include unlimited users (huge value)
2. **Modern UX** - Best-in-class user interface and mobile app
3. **International focus** - Strong multi-currency, global tax compliance
4. **App ecosystem** - 1,000+ integrations via Xero App Marketplace
5. **Cloud-native** - No desktop version, fully cloud-optimized
6. **Accountant-friendly** - Strong advisor/partner network globally

## Key Weaknesses

1. **Invoice limits** - Early plan limited to 20 invoices/month (forces upgrade)
2. **US market** - Smaller accountant network in US vs QuickBooks
3. **Inventory** - Inventory management less robust than QuickBooks Plus
4. **Project costing** - Only available on highest tier
5. **Learning curve** - Different terminology than US GAAP for American users

## Best Fit For

- **International businesses** or those with multi-currency needs
- **Growing teams** that need unlimited user access
- **Modern businesses** valuing clean UX and mobile access
- **Businesses outside North America** (stronger ecosystem internationally)
- **Companies with multiple collaborators** (unlimited users is killer feature)

## Competitive Position

- **vs QuickBooks:** Cleaner UX, unlimited users, better international; weaker US ecosystem
- **vs FreshBooks:** More comprehensive accounting, better for growth; less service-focused
- **vs Wave:** Full-featured paid solution with better reporting/automation
- **vs Sage Intacct:** Small business vs mid-market (Xero tops out around 100 users)

## Integration Highlights

- Stripe, PayPal, Square (payment processing)
- HubSpot, Salesforce (CRM)
- Shopify, WooCommerce, BigCommerce (e-commerce)
- Gusto, Deputy (payroll and HR)
- 1,000+ apps in Xero App Marketplace
- Open API for custom integrations

## Graduation Path

**When to outgrow Xero:**
- Multi-entity consolidation becomes complex (>5 entities)
- Revenue exceeds $20M or 100+ employees
- Need industry-specific ERP features (manufacturing, distribution)
- Require more advanced project accounting
- Need deeper financial planning/budgeting tools

**Next step:** Sage Intacct or NetSuite

## Notes

- Founded in New Zealand, strong in ANZ/UK/Europe
- Growing US presence but QuickBooks still dominant
- Unlimited users on ALL plans is unique value proposition
- Acquired Planday, WorkflowMax (vertical expansion)
- Strong focus on automation and efficiency
