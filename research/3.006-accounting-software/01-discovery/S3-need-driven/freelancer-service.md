**Last Updated:** October 22, 2025

## Scenario 1: Freelance Graphic Designer ($75K Revenue)

**Business Profile:**
- Solo graphic designer
- $75K annual revenue
- Bills clients hourly + project-based
- 10-15 clients per year
- Needs: Invoicing, expense tracking, time tracking, project profitability

**Primary Recommendation: FreshBooks Lite ($19/mo)**

**Rationale:**
- Built-in time tracking (automatically creates invoices from hours)
- Project tracking and profitability
- Beautiful client portal and proposals
- Simple enough for non-accountant
- Up to 5 billable clients fits perfectly

**Runner-Up: Wave Free**
- Pro: Free
- Con: No time tracking built-in

**Budget Alternative: Wave Free + Toggl**
- $0/mo (Wave) + $10/mo (Toggl for time tracking)
- More manual but works

**Red Flags:**
- Don't choose QuickBooks (overkill, too complex)
- Don't choose Sage Intacct (way too expensive)

**Migration Path:**
- Upgrade to FreshBooks Plus when clients >5
- At $150K+ revenue, consider QuickBooks if need more features

## Scenario 2: Marketing Agency ($500K Revenue, 8 Employees)

**Business Profile:**
- Digital marketing agency
- $500K annual revenue
- 8 employees (4 billable, 4 overhead)
- 25-30 active clients
- Needs: Time tracking, project budgets, client retainers, profitability by project

**Primary Recommendation: FreshBooks Plus ($33/mo) + Time Tracking**

**Rationale:**
- Excellent project management for agencies
- Time tracking built-in (critical for billable hours)
- Client portal for transparency
- Project profitability reporting
- Up to 50 clients supported

**Runner-Up: QuickBooks Plus + TSheets**
- More comprehensive accounting
- Better if need inventory or advanced features
- ~$85/mo + $40/mo (TSheets) = $125/mo total
- More complex to learn

**Budget Alternative: Zoho Books Professional ($50/mo)**
- Built-in time tracking
- Good project features
- Lower cost than FreshBooks + QB combo

**Red Flags:**
- Don't choose Wave (no time tracking, too basic)
- Don't choose Xero without time tracking add-on ($600+/year additional)

**Migration Path:**
- Stay with FreshBooks until revenue >$2M
- At $2M+, consider QuickBooks Advanced or Sage Intacct

## Scenario 3: Law Firm ($2M Revenue, 15 Attorneys + Staff)

**Business Profile:**
- Small law firm
- $2M annual revenue
- 15 attorneys, 10 staff
- Billable hours critical
- Trust accounting requirements
- Needs: Time tracking, matter/client tracking, trust accounting, IOLTA compliance

**Primary Recommendation: QuickBooks Online Advanced ($275/mo) + Legal-Specific Add-Ons**

**Rationale:**
- Can handle 25 users (15 attorneys + 10 staff)
- Matter-based tracking (classes/projects)
- Integrates with legal practice management software (Clio, MyCase)
- Most accountants familiar with QuickBooks
- Trust accounting add-ons available

**Runner-Up: Xero Established + Practice Management Integration**
- Unlimited users (huge value for 25 people)
- Good integrations with legal software
- $80/mo vs $275/mo savings
- Con: Fewer legal-specific features

**Enterprise Option: Sage Intacct + Legal Module**
- If revenue >$5M, consider Sage Intacct
- Better matter accounting and project tracking
- ~$25K/year (expensive but scales better)

**Red Flags:**
- Don't use FreshBooks (not designed for law firms)
- Don't use Wave (insufficient features for firm size)

**Migration Path:**
- QuickBooks Advanced until $5M revenue
- At $5M+, migrate to Sage Intacct with legal/professional services module

## Scenario 4: Consulting Firm ($5M Revenue, 35 Consultants, 3 Office Locations)

**Business Profile:**
- Management consulting firm
- $5M annual revenue
- 35 consultants across 3 offices (not legal entities, just locations)
- Complex project accounting
- Need dimensional reporting (by client, by office, by service line)
- CFO wants sophisticated financial reporting

**Primary Recommendation: Sage Intacct ($25K-35K/year)**

**Rationale:**
- Best-in-class project accounting
- Dimensional reporting (track by multiple dimensions)
- Scales to 35+ users easily
- Advanced time & expense
- Professional services focus
- Better than QuickBooks for this complexity

**Runner-Up: Xero Established + Projects Add-On**
- $960/year + $1,200/year (projects) = $2,160 total
- 90% cheaper than Sage Intacct
- Works if don't need advanced dimensions
- Con: Less sophisticated reporting

**Why NOT QuickBooks:**
- QuickBooks Advanced caps at 25 users (you have 35)
- Classes don't provide true dimensional reporting
- Performance issues at this transaction volume

**Red Flags:**
- Don't delay Sage Intacct too long (data migration gets harder)
- Don't choose NetSuite (don't need full ERP for consulting)

**Migration Path:**
- Sage Intacct is likely final platform
- Only upgrade to NetSuite if add product sales or manufacturing

## Scenario 5: Architectural Firm ($8M Revenue, 50 Employees, Project-Based)

**Business Profile:**
- Architecture firm
- $8M annual revenue
- 50 employees (architects, designers, admin)
- Project-based billing
- Needs: Job costing, percent-complete billing, retainage, AIA billing

**Primary Recommendation: Sage Intacct + Construction/AEC Module ($35K-50K/year)**

**Rationale:**
- Industry-specific construction/AEC features
- Job costing and project accounting
- Percent-complete revenue recognition
- Retainage tracking
- AIA billing format support
- Scales to 50+ users

**Runner-Up: Deltek Vision or Deltek Vantagepoint**
- Industry-specific (architecture/engineering focused)
- Similar price range to Sage Intacct
- Stronger on project management side
- Con: Accounting less robust than Sage Intacct

**Why NOT Small Business Platforms:**
- QuickBooks: Doesn't scale to 50 users, limited construction features
- Xero: Not designed for construction/AEC

**Red Flags:**
- Don't use general accounting software (need industry-specific)
- Don't choose NetSuite unless also doing product manufacturing

**Migration Path:**
- Sage Intacct or Deltek are endpoint platforms for this industry
- Rarely outgrow these

## Scenario 6: Nonprofit Organization ($3M Budget, 20 Staff, Grant Tracking)

**Business Profile:**
- 501(c)(3) nonprofit
- $3M annual budget
- 20 staff members
- Multiple grants and programs
- Needs: Fund accounting, grant tracking, donor management, program budgeting

**Primary Recommendation: Sage Intacct Nonprofit Edition ($20K-30K/year)**

**Rationale:**
- Industry-leading nonprofit fund accounting
- Grant and program tracking
- FASB compliance (ASC 958)
- Dimensional reporting by program/grant/fund
- Donor management integration (integrates with donor CRMs)
- Scales to $10M+ budget easily

**Runner-Up: QuickBooks Online Nonprofit Edition + Aplos**
- QuickBooks: $30-50/mo
- Aplos (nonprofit add-on): $100/mo
- Total: ~$150/mo ($1,800/year)
- Much cheaper than Sage Intacct
- Con: Less sophisticated fund accounting

**Budget Alternative: Aplos (Standalone Nonprofit Software)**
- $79-119/mo
- Built for nonprofits from ground up
- Fund accounting included
- Donor management included
- Con: Smaller ecosystem, less known to accountants

**Red Flags:**
- Don't use standard QuickBooks without nonprofit features
- Don't use Wave (no fund accounting)

**Migration Path:**
- Aplos/QuickBooks Nonprofit: $0-$2M budget
- Sage Intacct Nonprofit: $2M-$50M+ budget
- NetSuite Nonprofit: $50M+ budget (rare)

## Key Insights for Service Businesses

### Time Tracking is Critical
- FreshBooks has best built-in time tracking
- QuickBooks requires TSheets add-on ($40+/mo)
- Xero requires third-party add-on ($600+/year)
- Sage Intacct includes time tracking

### Project Profitability Matters
- FreshBooks: Excellent for simple project tracking
- QuickBooks: Good with projects/classes
- Sage Intacct: Best for complex multi-dimensional project accounting

### Scalability by User Count
- Wave, FreshBooks: Up to 10 users practical
- QuickBooks: Up to 25 users (Advanced tier)
- Xero: Unlimited users (huge value for service firms)
- Sage Intacct: 50-500+ users

### When to Graduate to Mid-Market
**Signals:**
- Revenue >$5M
- Users >25 (and QuickBooks won't work)
- Need sophisticated project accounting
- CFO wants dimensional reporting
- Multi-entity or complex structure
